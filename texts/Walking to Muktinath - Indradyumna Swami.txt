"It was a canopy style—we were all together, and it was very pleasant. It was very cold there in the mountains, and in fact, it was specifically covered with a thin layer of snow made of thick ice. We walked in the footsteps of the previous ladies; they were dressed very warmly, wearing hats and crystalline-like clothing. 

Muktinātha is very high in the mountains; it is such a holy place with 108 springs. People go there, and if you wash in all these springs—*aṣṭā-kālapūjā*—you can easily get liberation. Even the gods, when they were with us recently, ran around three times, bathed in all the springs. The elder devotees were justified, and I went with them. 

When we were there, some *sādhu* approached us. He descended from the mountain above; he walked through the snow and came up to me and said, "I offer my respects to you. I am a servant of the people, and I am a devotee of Nārāyaṇa." We discussed this moment, but then I looked down and saw that he was standing barefoot on the ice. Moreover, the ice was melting under his feet. He had such a level of energy in his body that he melted the ice with his own feet. Of course, this impresses people, but nevertheless, it does not impress us because we understand that the highest goal is to develop pure love for God. As explained while you are there, the common activity for all people is what helps us develop love for the Supreme Lord, and in order to completely liberate the soul, such activity must be selfless and continuous.""
