"Hare Kṛṣṇa, Hare Kṛṣṇa, Kṛṣṇa Kṛṣṇa, Hare Hare, Hare Rāma, Hare Rāma, Rāma Rāma, Hare Hare.

Welcome, everyone, on this wonderful, auspicious day. Especially, we want to welcome our guests. We apologize for the hot, scorching sun; we did not expect so many people today. This is a rather modest space, so please excuse me and be patient with the sun's rays. 

As you all know, today we are accepting candidate disciples for our ISKCON, our big international family of the International Society for Kṛṣṇa Consciousness—the family of Śrīla Prabhupāda. Once, Śrīla Prabhupāda said that the origin is not different from him, and he also said that we will have our own origin in the spiritual sky. Once, in a confidential conversation with my godbrother, he even said that upon returning to the spiritual world, we will all recognize each other. He said, "Well, you will not recognize me in the body of an old man; I will be a little boy." This is our family, our eternal family. According to tradition, when a girl leaves her parents' home and comes to her husband's house, she also takes his last name. For example, my mother's last name was "Er," and when she married my father, she changed her last name to "Eer." This is the tradition. In our country, when accepting disciples, a teacher gives a new name. Today, the spiritual teacher gives initiation, *dīkṣā*, and the chanting of the holy name.

This morning, I was thinking about the fact that by giving the holy name to these disciples—and this was given to all of us—we receive the greatest precious treasure in all fourteen worlds. All people are enchanted by treasure. Several years ago, there was a popular book and also a TV series or movie called *Treasure Island*. People went crazy about it; everyone dreamed of how they would go to an exotic desert island and find treasure. This morning, I was thinking about this. I turned to the dictionary, I looked for the definition of the word "treasure," and with all due respect, I want to say that this definition shows how little people know about real treasure. The official definition of the word "treasure" is a collection of valuable ancient items, such as gold coins and precious stones, that were hidden or lost. In the *Bhāgavatam*, gold is described as "yellow excrement." This is not the most elegant definition, but it is the definition for the general public. 

But for us devotees, we know and we have experienced that the real treasure is love for God, *kṛṣṇa-prema*. Otherwise, how do you explain that we all started some time ago, several years ago, chanting the Kṛṣṇa Mahā-mantra and still continue to chant the same mantra? There must be something special in it. You judge the value of an object by how much benefit you can extract from it. Of course, gold brings fleeting pleasure. There is nothing wrong with decorating yourself with gold and presenting yourself properly before Hari, Guru, and Vaiṣṇavas. In fact, even in the scriptures, it is mentioned that wearing gold helps a person reduce lust, just as wearing pearls helps calm an unquenchable mind and wearing sapphires helps keep evil spirits at bay. But such are the fleeting benefits that all these objects bring. As for us, we can talk for hours about the benefits this chanting bestows: it cleanses the heart of karma that has accumulated life after life and ultimately gives us wisdom—what is love for God. 

This chanting is so powerful that even walking down the street, accidentally hearing this chanting, a person receives benefit and to some extent his heart is purified. Once, I visited our temple in Paris and we all went to *harināma* on the street in front of a large store. At that moment, a woman passing by stood in front of us and showed us her tongue in an offensive manner. She covered her ears with her fingers, and in such a position, she stood there for 45 minutes listening to us. Later, I said everything was wonderful—it was a wonderful *harināma*—but there was such an incident. When I mentioned the woman who stood there with her hands over her ears, my godbrother asked, "What did you do in that case?" I said, "I didn't even know how to answer." He said, "You should have gone up to her, pulled your fingers out of her ears, and loudly chanted the Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra in her ear."

But of course, the benefits that chanting gives far exceed the purification of karma or the purification of material desires. In the end, it gives us *Kṛṣṇa-prema*. But this happens only through the process of initiation. We are here in this very place, not far from the birthplace of Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu. This is a real Treasure Island; here are nine islands, and each of them is a truth. In several places, this process is described; for example, the *Caitanya-caritāmṛta* describes it as a story. A person turns to an astrologer after looking at his natal chart. The astrologer told him that his father, who recently left this world, left him a huge treasure, and all he needed to do was go and dig it up. The astrologer warns him that he should not search and try to dig up this treasure in the south, north, or west; he must go to the east. The man was misled and asked, "Well, why can’t I go to the south, north, or west?"

The astrologer explained to him that if you go to the south, you will be bitten by wasps; if you go to the west, you will be haunted by ghosts; and if you go to the north, you will be swallowed by a snake. The man was confused and asked for an explanation. The astrologer explained that this countless treasure is the prime love of God. Love for God cannot be achieved by ritual activities that are popular among those who practice *karmī* activities. The Vedas describe paths for different types of people, even those who want to engage in sensual pleasures in a civilized way and not go to hell. If you follow this path, over time you can go to the heavenly planets. However, we know that from the higher planets in the material world to the lower ones, all these places are the abode of suffering where the cycle of birth and death never ends. So, it is not advised to follow this part of the Vedas.

Then the person asked, "Why can't I go west to America, to Silicon Valley, where I can study computers, be a computer engineer, and earn a ton of money?" There is nothing wrong with that, but for now, we are talking about *prema*, and that is different. The astrologer said because you will be haunted by ghosts. What are ghosts? Mental speculation. In the West, there is no such extensive knowledge about God. People begin to speculate and create their own versions of the universe—that God does not exist. We can say that atheists are actually possessed by ghosts. Of course, many, even most people, recognize the greatness of God, but their knowledge may be insufficient. Therefore, how can you develop love for God? How can you love the One you know very little of? We need knowledge about the Supreme Personality of Godhead to truly fall in love. Therefore, we are very grateful to the previous *ācāryas* who gave us so much information about Kṛṣṇa. 

We just finished a series of lectures: "Stimulation for Love." The last three lectures were about Kṛṣṇa's nose. Usually, the nose is not the most attractive part of the body, but in this case, Kṛṣṇa's nose deserves three lectures. And this Friday, there will be a lecture about Kṛṣṇa's chin. Everything about Kṛṣṇa's extraordinary form, *vigraha*, is not made of material elements; it is woven from eternity, knowledge, and bliss. As for the details regarding Kṛṣṇa, Prabhupāda said that we know a lot about God—we even have His "phone number," the *Hare Kṛṣṇa* mantra. Once, my spiritual brother and I were in Geneva, and we were talking about the need to keep up with this holy name and gain knowledge. In this way, it will be possible to develop love for Him.

In our story, the man asks the astrologer, "Why can't I go to the north and dig up my treasures there?" The astrologer answered, "Because yogīs who practice mystical practices live in the north, and they are eager to develop *siddhis*, or mystical powers." I was thinking about the mystical abilities that yogīs develop and came to the conclusion that they are all just a game with material elements. One can become lighter than light, heavier than heavy, or reach an object from a long distance, but all this is just movement and playing with material energy—just like a child plays with toys. It's innocent, but there is nothing transcendental about it. I hope we are not insulting the yogīs; we are simply not attracted to mystical abilities that play with material energy in a magical way. Prabhupāda told a story about a man who came to the bank of a river planning to cross it on a boat. He saw a mystical being who could walk on water. The man asked the yogī, "How long did it take you to develop such abilities?" The yogī answered, "It took me five lives." The man replied, "Yes, and I can just pay 20 rubles and swim across the river." Such abilities cannot be compared with the benefits that chanting gives us. Chanting gives us direct love for God.

I was reading books and found a story that is told in *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam*. It is a historical fact. Once, on the land of India, there was a warrior, King Māndhātṛ, who marched with his army and came across one of the saints of that place, Śrī Rāmānuja. This king asked the holy man, "Tell me where I can get and dig up the most precious treasures." *Ācāryas* always speak only the truth, whether it is pleasant or unpleasant. This holy man told him the truth: "I know where you can find the most magnificent treasure. It is nearby in the city of Śrī Raṅgam." He meant the treasure of love for God, which a person could find by worshiping the deity Raṅganātha in this temple and chanting His names. This king had a secretary who wrote down all his dialogues. In Raṅgam, there is still this document in which the holy man describes the treasure with hidden meaning. The holy man said, "If you come with me, I will show you the treasure. It is located between two rivers and is hidden behind seven walls. A huge snake guards this treasure." 

Let's analyze this. This temple is located between two rivers; Raṅgam is an island on one side of which is the Kāverī River, and on the other, the Kollidam. As for protection, seven walls have been erected around this place for many centuries, and the snake is Ananta-śeṣa. The treasure was erected by special rituals, and it can only be opened by chanting secret mantras—the *Oṁ namo nārāyaṇāya* mantra—and with the help of a special rare healing herb that carries rare virtues, not simple animal sacrifices. What is this healing herb of rare virtues? It means purity, truthfulness, mercy, and asceticism—the fundamental principles of religion. The sage hints that you need to acquire these virtues to acquire the treasure. He said in conclusion that this innumerable treasure of love for God can be found, and you will become the richest man on earth. If you find this treasure, you will be able to destroy any of your enemies—lust, anger, and greed. And it is said that the king followed the instructions, he went, received God, communicated with the saints, chanted the holy names, and ultimately became a devotee himself.

In our Gauḍīya tradition, we have similar stories. We know the story when the treasure boy turned to the *sādhus* and received the "desire stone." Over time, the boy realized that the real treasure and the most precious stone is the chanting of the holy name. Even a novice devotee can feel this power; it manifests through personal experience. Every devotee can feel this sweetness. We know that the *Bhagavad-gītā* says that a person can only leave something and receive something better in return, and we receive the chanting of the holy name. We must hold on to it, hold on to our *japa*, and continue chanting. If we do not do this, we will fall under the influence of illusion.

At the end of the lecture, we will read a verse that will save us from the danger of material energy, gross and subtle, and inspire us to chant:

"Renunciation and worldly talk are like a prostitute stealing all the savings from her lover; they steal a person's pure mind. You must unhesitatingly reject any talk of liberation, which, like a bloodthirsty tigress, devours your soul. Moreover, give up attachment even to Lakṣmī, the Śrī Nārāyaṇa, for this attachment leads to bondage. Live in Vraja, worship Śrī Śrī Kṛṣṇa, who gives the devotees the priceless treasure of love for Them."

Thank you very much.""
