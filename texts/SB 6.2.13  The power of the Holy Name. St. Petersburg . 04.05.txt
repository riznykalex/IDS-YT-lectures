"# SB 6.2.13: The Power of the Holy Name **Location:** St. Petersburg **Date:** 04.05

---

### Lecture Content

Good evening, Vaishnavas. Hare Kṛṣṇa. Hare Kṛṣṇa. 

This lecture is aimed at followers of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness. It does not pursue the goal of involvement in any other religious association and is not missionary activity. We continue our series of lectures on the life and activities of Ajāmila, and we are reading *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam* 6.2.13:

> *oṁ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya*
> *oṁ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya*
> *oṁ namo bhagavate vāsudevāya*
 **Translation:** 
"At the time of death, Ajāmila helplessly and very loudly pronounced the holy name of the Lord, Nārāyaṇa. Due to the fact that he pronounced it once, it freed him from all the reactions of his sinful activities. Thus, the servants of Mahārāja Yama did not try to take him, as he was freed from punishment and hellish conditions of life."

The Viṣṇudūtas, who were endowed with much greater power than the Yamadūtas, ordered them to release Ajāmila because, unlike them, they knew that Ajāmila had already been forgiven for all sins and should not suffer in hell. When Ajāmila pronounced the name "Nārāyaṇa," he called his son, but the holy name has enormous transcendental power. Naturally, the science of liberation confirms this. Kṛṣṇa states in *Bhagavad-gītā* (7.28):

> "Persons who have acted piously in previous lives and in this life, whose sinful actions are completely eradicated, and who are freed from the dualities of delusion, engage themselves in My service with determination."

Without getting rid of the reactions of sins, it is impossible to rise to devotional service. He who dies remembering Kṛṣṇa at that moment will immediately return home to Godhead. On the threshold of death, Ajāmila, in despair, loudly repeated the holy name of the Lord, Nārāyaṇa. This alone freed him from the reactions of all sins. Therefore, O servants of Yamarāja, do not dare take him away to your formidable master; Ajāmila has no place in hell.

### The Life of Ajāmila
Yesterday, we discussed the life story of Ajāmila. We covered his life from the cradle to the grave, and despite the fact that for most of his life he led a sinful life, at the time of death, he was fortunate enough to utter the holy name of Nārāyaṇa. It is natural that due to his sinful life, at the time of death, the Yamadūtas came to him to take him to hell to atone for his sins. The scriptures say that death comes to sinners, and naturally, the Yamadūtas arrived. But to their surprise, the Viṣṇudūtas appeared there, turned to the Yamadūtas, and said that there was no question of taking him to hell because he had uttered the holy name of Nārāyaṇa. 

The Yamadūtas, for the first time in millions of years, encountered an obstacle. They stood before these shining Viṣṇudūtas and argued. If two parties to a dispute cannot agree, it is resolved when an authority appears. The Viṣṇudūtas turned to them and said, "Go to Yamarāja, whose authority we also accept because he is one of the twelve *mahājanas*." To shorten the story: they went to Yamarāja, who confirmed that whoever utters the name of my dear Lord does not go to hell.

### Practice and Devotion
Therefore, in this life, we practice repeating the holy names so that at the moment of death, this happens naturally. In English, there is an expression: "Practice makes perfect." Our wonderful violinist, Haripriya Sundari, who is traveling with us, is superb. It is no coincidence that she constantly practices. She is so talented that she can play along no matter what melody I play. It looks easy, but she has many years of training; if you look at her fingers, they are covered with calluses from constant practice. 

In order to remember the Lord at the moment of death, it is necessary to practice thoroughly—this is called *sādhana-bhakti*. As we discussed yesterday, after Ajāmila was released, he went to Haridwar to the bank of the Ganges. There, he performed penance and consciously repeated the holy names. Before, he repeated his son’s name unconsciously, but on the bank of the Ganges, he concentrated fully. He left his body repeating the names of Nārāyaṇa; he did not call his son. The Yamadūtas did not come; the Viṣṇudūtas came on a golden chariot and took him away to the abode of Kṛṣṇa.

When I read such *līlā*, I know the end will be wonderful. This is not a fairy tale; this is the absolute truth. When a conditioned soul leaves the body, he will meet either the Yamadūtas or the Viṣṇudūtas. The Yamadūtas look very scary—something reminiscent of the zombies seen in modern movies. I once flew from Dubai next to a teenager watching a movie with creepy creatures. I asked him who they were, and he said, "These are wonderful zombies." I thought, "Those are more like Yamadūtas." They are so scary that people often lose control of their bodily functions at the moment of death. I asked a surgeon if this happens, and he confirmed it. But the Viṣṇudūtas are very beautiful and possess *siddha-deha*—the form of the body that resembles the Lord. This is not fiction; we accept this from *Guru, Śāstra,* and *Sādhu*.

### Faith and Reality
This is a question of your faith, which can be strengthened by correct association. When they first landed on the moon in 1969, an American astronaut had been there. One devotee with doubts came to Śrīla Prabhupāda’s room holding a newspaper and said, "Look, Prabhupāda, they went to the moon, took pictures, and no one lives there—there are only stones." He challenged his spiritual master. 

Prabhupāda had a copy of *Caitanya-caritāmṛta* on his desk. He opened it and said, "Scientists say the moon is made of stones, but the *Śāstra* says it is a heavenly planet where pious living beings reside, with beautiful rivers and nectar." He asked, "Which one should we accept? The conclusion of modern men associated with sinful actions, or the description of the pure devotee of the Lord?" Prabhupāda told him to throw away the newspaper, and the sheets flew everywhere. He said, "We will discard these stupid conclusions. We accept the Vedic descriptions as pure truth."

### The Most Important Day
I once met a devotee in Vṛndāvana who told me, "Indradyumna, do you know what the most important day of your life is? It is the day of your death." We Westerners think it is our birthday, our graduation day, or our wedding day. But actually, it is the day of death. I thought about how the day of my death determines the next life, which will last for hundreds of years. This is very important. 

A group of psychologists studied deaths during the war and found that soldiers' last words were almost always the names of their mothers. So, what we do in Kṛṣṇa consciousness is try to transfer all our love to Lord Kṛṣṇa. When death comes, we will chant Hare Kṛṣṇa. The material energy tests us—if you are cooking and burn yourself with hot ghee, do you say, "Oh, God!" or do you say, "Hare Kṛṣṇa"? One day, I saw a girl prick her finger with a needle; she was about to say something bad, but then caught herself and said, "Hare Kṛṣṇa, Mahārāja." We are tested because, at the moment of death, we will not have a second chance. 

### Quality of Chanting
This is not about the number of rounds, but their quality. Was your chanting the most joyful event of your day? Śrīla Prabhupāda said the most important instruction is to chant sixteen rounds of the *mahā-mantra* daily. If you do this, it should be the highest moment of your day. 

If you chant sixteen rounds, you pronounce 1,728 names of Kṛṣṇa. If you do this 365 days a year, that is over 600,000 holy names. If you manage this for 60 years, that is over 36 million holy names. If you do this with concentration, think of the benefit! When death knocks on your door, you will say with dignity, "Hare Kṛṣṇa, I knew you would test me, Mahārāja. I am ready."

Pure devotees are not afraid of death because they know they will gain liberation, and fools are not afraid because they think they can cheat death. Prabhupāda told a story of a rich businessman who thought he could bribe death, and another about a sculptor who tried to hide among his own statues, but death—who is very intelligent—tricked him by criticizing his art, causing his false ego to reveal himself. 

### Conclusion
As devotees, we should not try to cheat death, because death for us is finding a better position. Everything in Kṛṣṇa consciousness only gets better. Śrīla Prabhupāda wrote to a godbrother, Janānanda, who had left his body: "Kṛṣṇa was so kind to you that He took your sick body and gave you a body capable of continuing your service to Him."

We have many concessions and indulgences in this age of Kali. We should not have any excuse not to develop our Kṛṣṇa consciousness. We must gratefully accept the mercy of Śrīla Prabhupāda. Even if this process seems simple, if we are sincere and share our knowledge, at the time of death, we will not see zombies. We will see the golden chariot descending from the sky to help us reach Vaikuṇṭha. And when you see that chariot, you will raise your hands to meet it. 

Hare Kṛṣṇa.""
