It is by his grace that we find ourselves here today in one of the holiest places of Navadvīpa-dhāma. In particular, this place was one of the most beloved places of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. Yesterday, we started our parikramā at a brisk 2 PM and are still in it at its fullest. Ground Narasimha-pali is also part of it, and this is also a highlight.

Now, the mandir on the bank is the place where the forest of Vṛndāvana is situated. In Navadvīpa-dhāma, Mahāprabhu sings, "Oh my dear mind, please just worship this forest." We are sitting in this beautiful forest. It is said that by worshiping the forest of Vṛndāvana, you can directly see the pastimes of Caitanya Mahāprabhu, and they will emerge before your eyes. Yesterday, we heard about the glory of the dust. One can go into the deep meditation of the glory of the forest and the trees. We can pray to these trees, respecting them. The basis of life was never inferior; we stand for the common good and bhakti. Bhaktivinoda Ṭhākura describes that by worshiping these trees and showing them respect, we will receive the qualification to directly contemplate the pastimes of Mahāprabhu. 

I wanted to describe how Nityānanda Prabhu brought Jiva Gosvāmī here and what their state of mind was. The three of them—Nityānanda, Jiva Gosvāmī, and Śrīnivāsa Ācārya—came here. Nityānanda Prabhu visited this place, and those of you who know what happened in the Tota-gopinātha temple know that seeing the intensity of emotion, all three of them began to sob. Jiva Gosvāmī, in separation from the Lord, began to cry and beg Him to appear before him. Śrīnivāsa fainted from the realization of how magnanimous the Lord was, and Nityānanda Prabhu also cried and prayed to the Lord that He would appear before them. How could the Lord resist their desire? In fact, by that time, the Lord had already curtailed His pastimes on this planet. But at that moment, He appeared along with all His companions right in front of Lord Nityānanda and Jiva Gosvāmī. They recognized the Lord in His original form, and there, the Lord, along with all His companions—even with those who at that time had already left this planet—danced and sang for a long time.

After the Lord completed the manifestation of this līlā, we can imagine the state of mind of Lord Nityānanda and Jiva Gosvāmī. Lord Nityānanda, along with everyone else, began to walk to this place. He realized how dear this place was to Mahāprabhu Himself, and he couldn't even clearly pronounce words; he was simply immobilized. He couldn't move; he couldn't say anything. The only thing he could do was cry and chant the names of Gaurāṅga. At some point, all the devotees began to cry. Jiva Gosvāmī and Śrīnivāsa also began to sob. 

Nityānanda Prabhu remembered how Mahāprabhu contemplated this forest. Jagadānanda Paṇḍita once came here, and this place reminded the Lord of Śrī Vṛndāvana. When He was in Vṛndāvana, He saw a parrot here. He grabbed this parrot and said, "Oh son of the forest of Vṛndāvana, you are so lucky, you are in Vṛndāvana. Please chant Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa." That parrot looked at the Lord and began to repeat, "Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa." The external Lord was confused, saying, "This is Vṛndāvana-dhāma, chant Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa," and he let go of the parrot. The parrot answered him that the same Vṛndāvana has now appeared as Navadvīpa-dhāma, and the same Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa have now appeared before me as Gaura-Hari. Then He turned and looked at Gadādhara Paṇḍita and said, "And here is Rādhārāṇī herself." 

It is in this place that Lord Śiva eternally resides. Lord Śiva distinguishes this place as one that gives the liberation that a person who lived in Benares for thousands of years receives. A devotee can simply push away this liberation; one day of chanting the name of Gaura in this place is greater. Another glorification is that any person leaving his body here, before he leaves, Lord Śiva will come and chant in his ear, "Gaurāṅga." This is exactly what Lord Nityānanda told Jiva Gosvāmī. 

A river used to flow here; now it is no longer visible. On that bank, there is Alakānanda, and the part of the river which is closer to us is Mandākinī. The far part is Yamunā and the near part is Gaṅgā. Exactly the same way here, the passage flows as Alakānanda and Mandākinī. And we have Benares, where Lord Śiva lives. This is the expansion of the original Benares. Here lives the beautiful deity Hāri-Hāra. Two hundred and forty-two years ago, King Kṛṣṇa Chandra Roy established this temple. He did this for the reason that in his kingdom, there were constant riots, and people argued among themselves who is the highest: Hari (the Supreme Lord) or Hara (Lord Śiva). In order to glorify them both, he installed this deity Hāri-Hāra. 

Several years ago, this deity was stolen. The thief picked up this deity, walked only a few steps, and then could no longer carry it, so he left it in the bushes. In the morning, jackals came and began to run back and forth from these bushes, making sounds. Local residents realized the jackals were trying to tell them something, so they went into the bushes, saw the deity, and informed the police. The police took it to their station, and later, the devotees brought luxurious offerings to the police station, and after that, they returned the deity here. We can see that on the side of Hari sits Lakṣmī Devī, and on the side of Hara sits Pārvatī Devī. This shows how Mahāprabhu respected Advaita Ācārya, as Hari-Hara is indistinguishable from Advaita Ācārya, because Advaita Ācārya is the incarnation of Mahāviṣṇu and Sadāśiva.

One day, when all the devotees gathered together with Advaita Ācārya, Mahāprabhu turned to Śrīnivāsa and asked, "Who was Advaita Ācārya?" Śrīnivāsa thought for a while and answered, "I think he could have been either Śukadeva Gosvāmī or Prahlāda Mahārāja." When the Lord heard this, He got very angry and slapped him. The Lord said, "How can you compare Advaita Ācārya with Prahlāda Mahārāja or Śukadeva Gosvāmī? They are like children who were born just yesterday compared to Advaita Ācārya." With such a pastime, the Lord glorified Advaita Ācārya. 

This place is extremely important to us because without the grace of Lord Śiva, we have no hope of gaining anything. We can pray to Lord Śiva that wherever we are during the time of leaving the body, he, by his mercy, comes and chants in our ear the name of Gaurāṅga. At the request of Nityānanda Prabhu, we can worship this forest, and by the grace of this forest, one day we will be able to contemplate the pastimes of Mahāprabhu. 

I pray to Lord Nityānanda, who opened the market of the Holy Name, to give liberation to all conditioned souls. I pray for three things: chant Hare Kṛṣṇa, worship Kṛṣṇa more, and teach others. Be careful and do not commit offenses. Just take the Holy Name of Kṛṣṇa; Kṛṣṇa is your mother, your father, and the treasure of your whole life. Leaving all sinful activity, continue all your worldly activities only in relation to Kṛṣṇa, and also show compassion to other conditioned souls by loudly chanting the names of Kṛṣṇa. This is the essence of all forms of religion.""
