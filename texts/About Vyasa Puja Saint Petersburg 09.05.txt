"Today, we gather to celebrate Vyāsa-pūjā. According to tradition, we should first explain what this holiday is. We know from the Vedic scriptures that there was a legendary compiler or editor of Vedic works named Kṛṣṇa-dvaipāyana Vyāsa, and all spiritual teachers follow the disciplic succession from him. Today is called Vyāsa-pūjā because we worship the representative of Vyāsadeva.

It is important to understand why this happens. The word *vyāsa* itself has several meanings in Sanskrit: the compiler, the one who explains, the one who divides. The prefix *vi-* indicates concretization, and *āsa* relates to activity; that is, one who is engaged in the specific activity of explaining Vedic works. Although we know about the person whose name was Vyāsa, this is not just a person—it is a position. In different eras, different personalities occupy the position of Vyāsa to compose and explain Vedic works.

As explained by the scriptures, Vyāsadeva is considered a literary embodiment of the Supreme Lord. He does not belong to the category of a *jīva*, or an ordinary living being; he is a living being through whom the energy of the Supreme Lord passes—the energy of knowledge—and is fully empowered by that knowledge, what we call *śakti*. An *āveśa-avatāra* is one who is endowed with special powers and authority on behalf of the Supreme Lord. That is why any qualified person who is in the disciplic succession of Vyāsadeva occupies a position in which they are worshiped, and the process of worshiping this person is called *Vyāsa-pūjā*.

Therefore, we conduct the Vyāsa-pūjā ceremony for all spiritual teachers. Today we have a special day, celebrating His Holiness. The meaning of this holiday is to express our gratitude and appreciation for what the spiritual teacher gives. As we have heard, the most important gift of a spiritual teacher is the gift of transcendental knowledge. That is why Lord Kṛṣṇa recommends in Chapter 4 of the *Bhagavad-gītā* that we turn to a spiritual teacher.

Naturally, knowledge alone is not a sufficient qualification to occupy this position. In the *Upadeśāmṛta*, it is said of a spiritual teacher:

> *vāco vegaṃ manasaḥ krodha-vegaṃ jihvā-vegam udaropastha-vegam*
> *etān vegān yo viṣaheta dhīraḥ sarvām apīmāṃ pṛthivīṃ sa śiṣyāt*

"A sober person who can tolerate the urge to speak, the mind's demands, the action of anger, and the urges of the tongue, belly, and genitals is qualified to make disciples all over the world."

This does not happen because someone suddenly wakes up early in the morning and decides they will be the spiritual teacher of the whole world. It happens naturally when qualified devotees receive authority from the Supreme Lord, and the Lord, located in the hearts of those around them, inspires them to turn to that person as a spiritual teacher. This happened to everyone, including our spiritual teacher. As he himself says, he did not consider himself qualified at the very beginning, but ultimately he realized this is the service he wanted to perform. It is important for the spiritual teacher to fulfill the desire of the Supreme Lord. He performs this service, and the process that brings us closer to the spiritual teacher is called *tapaḥ*, the process of dedication. It is said that spiritual knowledge must be obtained while being in close proximity to the spiritual teacher. For example, in the Vedic works, one of the main terms is *Upaniṣad*, translated as "sitting next to the spiritual teacher." This is a very important aspect of developing a relationship with the spiritual teacher because *yasya prasādād bhagavat-prasādo*—by the grace of the spiritual teacher, we receive the mercy of the Supreme Lord.

In the *Gītā*, Kṛṣṇa says that he received knowledge from the spiritual teacher, but we must first ask relevant questions to clearly understand how to apply that knowledge in practice and then offer service. Offering service to the Supreme Lord through the transparent medium of the spiritual teacher is the most important method of spiritual development. Any devotee who takes seriously what the spiritual master says and applies this knowledge in practice will certainly develop spiritual experience. He will develop a relationship with Kṛṣṇa. His path will be determined, and he will certainly develop spiritual gratitude for what the spiritual teacher has given. No one else is capable of this. Naturally, the heart of such a sincere student will show gratitude, which they will want to express. An event like today is the best opportunity to express that appreciation and share the spiritual experience that is the result of serving the spiritual teacher.

I am sure that today you will hear confirmations that will help you understand how this relationship with Kṛṣṇa and the spiritual teacher develops through devotional service. Kṛṣṇa himself says to Arjuna that whoever considers himself a devotee of His devotee is actually His devotee. Our service through the spiritual teacher is much higher than if we offered it directly to the Lord, and this is logical because the spiritual teacher has achieved perfection in devotional service.

It is said that there are three types of avatars:
1. The *avatāra* when the Supreme Lord himself comes to this planet in various incarnations.
2. The *avatāra* in the form of a deity, *arcā-vigraha*, appearing on the altar.
3. The *avatāra* in the form of the *ācārya*, or the spiritual teacher.

It is said that the incarnation of the Supreme Lord in the form of a spiritual teacher is the most merciful—much more merciful than the avatar of the Supreme Lord in his original form. Why? Because to serve the Lord directly, very high qualifications are needed. For the *arcā-vigraha*, the Supreme Lord's deity form, it is not so easy for beginner devotees to understand that the Lord can enter into a relationship with them. If you are convinced that the deity is the Lord himself, He will talk to you; but if you think this is just a statue made of stone, wood, or metal, the Lord agrees with that perception and will remain just a statue for you. That is, to serve the deities and understand that this is the Supreme Lord, a high level of spiritual perfection is needed.

But when the Lord manifests in the form of the *ācārya*—the spiritual teacher—He is very accessible for communication. This is what everyone sincerely feels through the teachings: when we are in a state of ignorance, the spiritual teacher illuminates our path. By serving him, we can directly satisfy the Supreme Lord even more than if we were to try to serve Him directly. Such is the advantage of the relationship with the spiritual teacher. Therefore, this is the most important thing every sincere student focuses on: to satisfy the spiritual teacher and to develop a personal relationship with him. We have gathered here to express our gratitude, to ask for his blessings for further service and spiritual progress, and to glorify the sublime qualities of the spiritual teacher, for through such glorification, we ourselves develop.""
