"### Causeless Mercy: Gaur Pūrṇimā Lecture
 **Regarding the Gaura Pūrṇimā appearance:** You are all mountain *bhaktas*, and we glorify you because you are the companions of the Lord, Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu. In this age of Kali, intelligent people perform congregational chanting in order to worship the incarnation of the Lord who constantly chants the names of Kṛṣṇa. He is not black in color; He Himself is Kṛṣṇa, and He always travels with His companions, servants, and weapons.

This is a wonderful verse for such a holiday. This text is from *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam* (11.5.32). Śrīla Prabhupāda explains this verse in his commentary, where he says that Lord Kṛṣṇa also appears in a golden color. This golden Lord Kṛṣṇa is known as the Lord of *sankīrtana*, and He is worshiped by all intelligent people of this age. This is also confirmed in *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam* (10.8.13); it says that although the child Kṛṣṇa was black, He also appeared in three other colors: red, white, and yellow. He appears in white and red when He comes during Satya and Tretā yugas, but He does not appear in a golden color until He comes as the Lord of *sankīrtana*, who is also known as Gaurāṅga.

This wonderful verse describes much, and a lot can be said about the Lord of *sankīrtana*. The *Caitanya-caritāmṛta* describes Him in seventeen volumes. What can we say about Him briefly within an hour? But the main thing that we need to understand is that He is the *yuga-avatāra*—the incarnation of this age. As Prabhupāda says in his commentary, the Lord comes in every age to preach religion; He comes to present the *dharma* of the age. No one except the Lord can do this. All those incarnations that come into this world are unusual and unique, and each age is different; therefore, different incarnations come with a specific purpose suitable for that age. Out of tens of thousands, millions, and billions of incarnations, there is one that is unique, and that is Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, whose appearance day we celebrate today.
 **Why this incarnation is unique:** The *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam* gives us a hint as to why this incarnation is so important: "Amazing incarnation! You are Kṛṣṇa Himself, appearing as Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu in the golden color of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, and You are widely spreading the love of God. We all offer our deep obeisances to You."

The most important point is that this incarnation widely spreads the love of God (*prema*). Many incarnations have come, but their goal was not to spread *prema* exactly. When any incarnation comes, it concentrates on the main tasks: saving devotees, killing demons, and spreading the laws of religion. For example, to save devotees, the main incarnation was Lord Nṛsiṁhadeva. He came for a very short time—literally for a moment. He appeared from the column, killed Hiraṇyakaśipu on His knees, invited Prahlad to Himself, and after that, disappeared.

*   **Lord Varāhadeva:** Killed Hiraṇyākṣa.
*   **Lord Kapiladeva:** Established the principles of religion by preaching *Sāṅkhya*.

Is it mentioned anywhere that these incarnations spread *prema* (love for God)? Perhaps they talked about the process by which, after billions of lives, you will achieve perfection, but none of them concentrated exactly on *prema*. It is said that all glory to the Lord, because He, like no other, spread exactly the love for God; He filled the whole earth with love. This is also mentioned in the *Caitanya-caritāmṛta*. The Lord went from one city to another, performing *kīrtana*. Thus, the Lord has flooded this whole world, spreading the love of God. This happened 500 years ago, and since it is said that the mission of the Lord to spread *nāma-saṅkīrtana* will continue for 10,000 years, according to my calculations, we have 9,480 years left.
 **The Nature of Love:** Before Caitanya Mahāprabhu appeared, the love that the devotees experienced was, as a rule, love expressed in respect and reverence (*aiśvarya*). But Caitanya Mahāprabhu and His companions spoke about the love that exists in Vṛndāvana—the love for Kṛṣṇa, which is sweeter. You cannot experience this love for Lakṣmī-Nārāyaṇa; you can experience it only with Kṛṣṇa. Kṛṣṇa reveals His childish games, like the Damodara-līlā. It is very easy to love a child, and Kṛṣṇa is always surrounded by devotees who are in tears of love for Him. And the perfection of this love is how the *gopīs* of Vṛndāvana loved Him—this is called conjugal love (*madhurya-rasa*). You will not find this relationship in any other incarnation.
 **Causeless Mercy:** He who now distributes the highest love, *prema*, is not only for the sages but also for the most fallen. Rūpa Gosvāmī freezes with admiration and thinks, "God, what is happening now? *Prema* is distributed to everyone, the most fallen *jīvas*!" This is the expression "causeless mercy"—mercy for which there is absolutely no reason. It is the desire of the Lord to give everyone *prema*. 

The problem is that all *jīvas* came here to forget the Lord. As much as a devotee tries to remember God and love Him, a conditioned soul tries to forget Him. I remember how Prabhupāda said in a lecture that when we were leaving the spiritual world, Kṛṣṇa followed us for some time and said, "Dear soul, please don't leave Me, don't go." But we said, "No." Love is always spontaneous and mutual. That is why the Lord came here to spread love.
 **The "Lifeboat" of the Lord:** When Krishna comes to this world, many people deny Him. In the *Bhagavad-gītā*, there is a unique *śloka*: fools deny Him when He comes in human form; they do not realize His transcendental nature. Such people are called fools or asses. 

Once, a disciple of mine from the island of Tahiti came to receive initiation. She told me, "Guru Mahārāja, will you accept me? I have the heaviest karma. My great-great-grandmother used to capture English sailors, boil them in a cauldron, and eat them. The blood of a *rākṣasa* flows in me." I thought, "Well, I have to accept it." The only qualification for receiving initiation—the mercy of Caitanya Mahāprabhu—is that you are disappointed in your past sinful activities, undertake to chant 16 rounds of the *mahā-mantra*, and follow the regulative principles.
 **The Secret Weapon: *Sankīrtana*:** You might wonder, "Where is the Lord's weapon?" When we see Lord Nārāyaṇa, He has a lotus, *cakra*, and club. But the weapon that Caitanya Mahāprabhu uses is unusual—it is *sadhu-saṅga*, the communication with devotees. Once, He called for His Sudarśana-cakra, but Advaita Ācārya said, "Lord, You came to this age to punish the demons through Your compassion, thus liberating everyone. Please cancel this Sudarśana; let this disappear. You have a different mood." And then Lord Caitanya just hugged Advaita Ācārya and purified him. 

Prabhupāda always said that *sādhu-saṅga* is an amazing weapon. And *prasāda* is also a secret weapon. As we were told at the celebration of the appearance of our spiritual master, "The more you eat, the more advancement you make." I remember one day in France, when I went to deliver a *chādar* to Prabhupāda’s room, I saw him standing near the window, watching the devotees eating *prasāda*. He was completely absorbed in contemplating them, and tears were running down his cheeks. He said, "Let them taste *prasāda*. Let them take." At that moment, I understood that *prasāda* is not just food; it is a mercy that purifies the heart.
 **Conclusion:** Let your promise for this New Year be to increase the number of rounds and the quality of your *japa*. As the *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam* states, the result you received in the Satya-yuga by meditating on the Lord, or in the Tretā-yuga by performing sacrifices, or in the Dvāpara-yuga by worshiping the lotus feet of the Lord, can all be obtained in the age of Kali simply by chanting the Hare Kṛṣṇa *mahā-mantra*.

Hare Kṛṣṇa, Hare Kṛṣṇa, Kṛṣṇa Kṛṣṇa, Hare Hare.
Hare Rāma, Hare Rāma, Rāma Rāma, Hare Hare.""
