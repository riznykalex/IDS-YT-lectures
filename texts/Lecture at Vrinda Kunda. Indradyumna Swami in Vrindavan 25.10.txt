I am astonished at everyone's conclusion, but just now, as we were traveling northwest to the village, I noticed several installations and quite a few raspberries.

It is described that next to the kuṇḍa is where Vṛndā-devī lives. It is described how she, one of the three important yoginīs, arranged everything. You stand there and cook porridge in these special places. There are three most special places where Śrī Kṛṣṇa meets daily in the morning, and this place is the first where they feel so. At noon, Śrī Kṛṣṇa meets there personally. Even now, since this is eternal—this is the spiritual world—all this happens every day. We walk past all these pastimes; we don't see anything. All this happens in the unmanifested pastimes, but some devotees have the ability to see all these games that pass before their eyes, just as it was 5,000 years ago.

Now, if you go to that place next to the forest of Mahādeva, you'll see a big wide road with new stores, but in fact, there's a beautiful grove there. Beautiful flowers are blooming. It is said that Vṛndā-devī lives right next to the kuṇḍa. And today, we are right here. She is described as very beautiful, and her skin is golden. She wears blue clothes and always has a parrot on her hand, which is also called the messenger. In fact, she has an entire army at her disposal to manage the forest. Her birds fly all day, conveying various messages. She is a very important person, and even when a devotee actually works under the direction of the Supreme Lord, there is a whole system.

Recently, I saw very old paintings describing the pastimes of Vṛndā-devī. We know the famous līlā on Mānas-gaṅgā, and our artists also depicted them in their paintings; we can see there a beautiful boat of charming Kṛṣṇa. Vṛndā-devī is the dominant deity of internal energy, called Yogamāyā. She manifests herself as Subhadrā; they are engaged in monitoring everything that happens during the internal pastimes. Their main task is to ensure that the pastimes of Kṛṣṇa are rich in taste and relationships.

One devotee told how he always tried to penetrate that transcendental realm. He had to spend the whole night in meditation. In the early morning, they still meet with Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī. So, the question arises: how is it that we can find out that Kṛṣṇa is there? All this is because the goddess insidiously found a way to inform the devotees that Kṛṣṇa had arrived, and she set all this up so that the pastimes of the Lord would have an amazing taste. When you finally achieve what you wanted, it acquires a special sweetness.

Lalitā-devī-dasi explains that maybe it was just such a mood to complicate the meetings of the Lord, because it enhances the taste of the meeting. It's like chutney—when you eat a good meal, you add something very spicy, and it gives the food a special taste. Vṛndā-devī is responsible for everything in this entire scenario. She writes this novel; she is the screenwriter. This is the original, perfect, transcendental history written by Vṛndā-devī.

When she receives a script from Kṛṣṇa, she begins to make it come to life. What trees are needed? What flowers are needed? What color should they be? What birds should be there? What songs need to be sung, and in what rhythm? What bees will buzz? What breezes will blow? All this is very important when it comes to love. If we had to appoint someone for perfume, then of course it would be Vṛndā-devī, who buys the most exotic perfumes for the deities. She is the artist who stages these līlās.

There are dark nights when the moon is thin, and she dresses in dark clothes and darkens the whole forest so that Kṛṣṇa can meet unhindered. The inhabitants of the spiritual world are very generous and magnanimous. It is said that Vṛndā-devī experiences pleasure 10,000 times more when she engages someone in service than if she herself performed the service. Since she is responsible for the games of Kṛṣṇa not only in the spiritual world but also materially, she comes into the world of people. Serving Tulasī-devī allows us to get closer to the privilege of understanding Kṛṣṇa's pastimes.

We overcome all these asceticisms, we walk long distances, we steam in the heat; all this so that one day you and I can penetrate this transcendental atmosphere. What is the purpose of Kṛṣṇa consciousness? To live here forever. And how will we succeed? It will succeed by the grace of a devotee like Vṛndā-devī. If you can see these individuals, then you can penetrate into *nitya-līlā-praviṣṭa*, the eternal pastimes. Vṛndā-devī graciously spread herself throughout the world in the form of her Tulasī tree. When you get home—whether in Moscow, St. Petersburg, or Kyiv—you can turn to her with prayers and beg, and by her grace, be here in the pastimes.

Vṛndā-devī wrote a song—a prayer dedicated to the Lord. Please, merciful Tulasī-devī, make it so that I become one of your servants. Oh, shining and merciful river Gangā, I take shelter of your lotus feet. Because of this, amazing spiritual desires arose in my heart. My only hope is your causeless mercy. By your grace, I am perfect.

This is a wonderful prayer worth remembering. If you can't remember the exact words, then just repeat the queen of all mantras, the Mahā-mantra: *Hare Kṛṣṇa, Hare Kṛṣṇa, Kṛṣṇa Kṛṣṇa, Hare Hare / Hare Rāma, Hare Rāma, Rāma Rāma, Hare Hare*. This mantra is also a prayer: "My dear Kṛṣṇa, my dear Rādhārāṇī, please have mercy on me and allow me to engage in the service of the devotees." Repeat this prayer as often as possible when you are near the Tulasī plant. It is described that Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī Ṭhākura made sure that the Tulasī plant was always present there. As a fish cannot live without water, I cannot maintain my life in this body if I have forgotten how to serve Tulasī Mahārānī.""
