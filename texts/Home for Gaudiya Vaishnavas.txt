Hare Kṛṣṇa. We’ve been here in Māyāpur for two weeks—it’s been two weeks in Vṛndāvana relishing the *kathā* about the pastimes of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu and the various devotees and associates who served Him here. Actually, the same personalities who assisted Kṛṣṇa in His pastimes 5,000 years ago in Vṛndāvana, namely the Vraja-gopīs, took birth as male personalities to participate in Lord Caitanya’s *saṅkīrtana* movement, which was primarily to distribute the *yuga-dharma* of chanting the Hare Kṛṣṇa maha-mantra. 

So you could say that for Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇavas, this is our home. And there’s no place like home—isn’t there that saying, "There’s no place like home"? So we’ve come here to become purified and to become invigorated with *Gaura-kṛpā*—with Lord Caitanya’s mercy—so that we can go back to our *Prabhupāda-deśas*. *Prabhupāda-deśa* means that place in the world where you’re serving the mission of Caitanya Mahāprabhu, to fulfill His prediction that from this place, the Yogapīṭha here in Māyāpur, the chanting of Hare Kṛṣṇa will go to every town and village of the world. 

So I think it’s fitting that we finish our one-month *parikramā* here in Śrīdhāma Māyāpur. Our devotees have become so enlivened. They got *Kṛṣṇa-kṛpā* and they got *Gaura-kṛpā*, all by the mercy of *Prabhupāda-kṛpā*. We have more mercy than we can actually handle! 

One time, Prabhupāda’s father, Gour Mohan Dey, told little Abhay—our spiritual master when he was young—that the Supreme Personality of Godhead, the *virāṭ-rūpa*, the Lord as He is manifested in this cosmic creation, has hundreds of thousands of legs and arms and hands. "So if He wants to give you mercy with all of His hands, how much mercy can you take with your two little hands?" 

So we’re feeling like that at the end of our one-month Kārtika *parikramā*—that we’ve received so much mercy from so many saintly persons, from so many *kalpa-vṛkṣa* trees, from bathing in so many sacred *kuṇḍas* and so many rivers, from seeing so many merciful Deities, from taking so much merciful *prasādam* in so many holy places, so much *kīrtana*, so much chanting, just walking in the dust of these *dhāmas*. Like we say in Vṛndāvana: *vraja-reṇu*. Māyāpur is non-different from Vṛndāvana. So in the same way, by walking in the dust here where the feet of the Pañca-tattva danced in great ecstasy, inaugurating the *saṅkīrtana* movement—just walking barefoot—we have hopefully become purified, invigorated, and we're really hoping that one day, perhaps in some birth in the distant future, we'll get some *kṛṣṇa-prema*.

Our spiritual master, Śrīla Prabhupāda, wanted us to assist him in the mission of the *ācāryas* and Caitanya Mahāprabhu in spreading the *saṅkīrtana* movement all over the world. But it was also his desire that we become pure devotees and we ourselves go back—back home, back to Godhead—when he decides that it is fitting. So we hope we’ve imbibed more of the *saṅkīrtana* spirit, the heart of a Vaiṣṇava, which is to share his or her good fortune with others. And we also pray that we’ve come a little bit closer to that goal of developing *kṛṣṇa-prema*, pure, unalloyed love for the Supreme Personality of Godhead. 

These Kārtika *parikramās* are very pure, they’re very potent, they’re very powerful. We hope that the devotees who have been watching our *parikramā*—both the Vṛndāvana videos and the Māyāpur videos—we hope that it will give you a strong desire to come to India, to come to Vṛndāvana and Māyāpur in particular, and participate in *parikramā*. 

Actually, coming to the *dhāma*, living in the *dhāma*, or visiting the *dhāma* is one of the five *aṅgas*, or the five most potent activities of devotional service: chanting Hare Kṛṣṇa, hearing *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam*, associating with devotees, worshiping the Deity, and living in or visiting the holy *dhāmas*. So if you can, if you can manage your time, put it on your transcendental bucket list and come with us! We pray that Kṛṣṇa will give us the opportunity to take many, many more devotees for many more years on these Kārtika *parikramās*, and that this ultimately gives auspiciousness to the world.
