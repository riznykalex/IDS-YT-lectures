"I’d like to extend to everyone a personal invitation to come to "The Greatest Show on Earth." What is the greatest show on Earth? More implicitly, Kṛṣṇa’s Village of Peace at Woodstock. Just like they say every Muslim should go to Mecca once in their lifetime—you have to make the Hajj—wherever you are, Africa, Middle East, Australia, once in your lifetime every devotee must go to Mecca; so every Ukrainian devotee and Russian devotee must go to Woodstock, Kṛṣṇa’s Village of Peace, once in their life. 

This will be our 28th year. You know what it’s like. We have this huge spot almost in the middle. Every day we have kīrtana, lectures, and stage shows. 140,000 full plates of prasādam in 72 hours—it’s just a modern-day pastime of Lord Caitanya. You have to see it; there’s no reason you shouldn't. It will increase your faith in Mahāprabhu. 

Nothing lasts forever in this world, so while it’s going strong, like the great Woodstock Festival, the organizer told me, "You, Mahārāja, you do whatever you want; you have *carte blanche*," as we say in French. "You can do anything you want," and we do everything we want.

This year, you’re expecting 1 million young people. They’re expecting 1 million people, and we are right in the center of it all. It’s a very safe environment; there’s hardly any violence at the festival, none in our village. We have security and everything—super clean. And the accommodation, you know, you’re in a room with everybody lined up; if one person turns over at night, everybody else is like sardines in a can. 

It’s intense. We work hard, but the joy is not just like these festivals where we’re having fun; the joy is seeing all these new people come and dance like this. Because Bhakti-puruṣottama Mahārāja is there, I’m there sometimes, Indradyumna Mahārāja is always there—some of the best in ISKCON, they go to that festival like a bee goes to the flower for honey.""
