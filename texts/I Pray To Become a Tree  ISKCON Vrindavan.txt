### Translation and Purport by Śrīla Prabhupāda
 **The Supreme Personality of Godhead said:** "O greatest of lords, just see how these trees are bowing their heads at Your lotus feet, which are worshipable by the immortal demigods. The trees are offering You their fruits and flowers to eradicate the dark ignorance that has caused their birth as trees."
 **Purport:** The trees of Vṛndāvana were thinking that because of past offenses they had now taken birth as trees, and being immovable, could not accompany Lord Kṛṣṇa in His wanderings throughout the Vṛndāvana area. In fact, all the creatures of Vṛndāvana, including the trees and cows, were great souls who could personally associate with the Supreme Personality of Godhead. But because of ecstatic sentiments of separation, the trees considered themselves in ignorance and thus tried to purify themselves by bowing down at the lotus feet of Kṛṣṇa and Balarāma. Lord Kṛṣṇa, understanding their mentality, simultaneously danced at them with affection and praised their devotional service before His older brother.

Hare Krishna. In this verse of the *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam*, we hear Lord Kṛṣṇa glorifying the trees in Vṛndāvana. In *Kṛṣṇa* book, chapter 22, Kṛṣṇa also praises the trees in Vṛndāvana with very beautiful words. He is addressing His cowherd boyfriends on the banks of the Yamunā. He says, "My dear friends, just look at these most fortunate trees of Vṛndāvana. They have dedicated their lives for the welfare of others. Individually, they are tolerating all kinds of natural disturbances such as hurricanes, torrents of rain, scorching heat, and piercing cold. Nevertheless, they are very careful to relieve our fatigue and give us shelter. They are like a noble person who has sacrificed everything possible—the body, mind, activities, intelligence, and words—for the welfare of all living entities." 

Kṛṣṇa is glorifying the trees, and Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī, in his illustrious *Vṛndāvana Mahimāmṛta*, goes so far as to say: "I bow down before the great trees of Vraja, which are filled with leaves and sprouts, maddened bees, the tumult of many birds, streams of nectarian honey, and branches bowed down with many splendid, wonderful fruits and flowers. I pray to become a tree in Śrī Vṛndāvana and please Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa with my limitless, splendid, sweet fruits, my unlimitedly splendid fragrant flowers filled with splendid pollen and splendid honey, my splendid hosted birds, and my splendid twigs and buds. Oh, let me become a tree in Vṛndāvana." 

So, with these words of the Lord and Śrīla Bhaktisiddhānta Sarasvatī, we should never consider the trees in Vṛndāvana to be ordinary. They are extraordinary. 

In fact, as all of us know, they are addressed as *kalpa-vṛkṣa* trees—desire trees. Actually, they are all *nitya-siddhas*. They are all eternally liberated souls who each have a unique *rasa* with Kṛṣṇa. Of course, it is an interesting *rasa*; it is a *rasa* of neutrality, actually. But we shouldn't think for a minute that the *rasa* of neutrality is anything less than the *rasa* of servitorship, friendship, parental love, or even conjugal love. No, the trees' *rasa* with Kṛṣṇa is just as pleasing to Him as any other *rasa*. In fact, all the *rasas*—as we learn from the Gosvāmīs—in Vṛndāvana are to facilitate the *mādhurya-rasa* of Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa. 

In *Govinda-līlāmṛta* by Kṛṣṇadāsa Kavirāja Gosvāmī, he writes that just as devotees in *dāsya*, *sakhya*, *vātsalya*, and *mādhurya-rasa* experience eight symptoms of ecstatic love for Kṛṣṇa, so do those in the mood of neutrality, such as the trees. That is very interesting. In the various higher *rasas*, they experience the eight symptoms of ecstatic love, but so do the trees. And what is that stimulus for the ecstatic symptoms that the trees sometimes exhibit? Where do they get their stimulus? Just like we have been lecturing for several years now on stimulation for ecstatic love: there are so many things that stimulate this ecstatic love in Śrī Vṛndāvana—it is almost endless. 

So what is the stimulus that creates these ecstatic symptoms in trees? Well, the ācāryas say one is the very fact that they are growing in the *raja*. They are growing in the dust of Vṛndāvana.
