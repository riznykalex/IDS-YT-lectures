I really like to travel, especially when the journey is connected with the search for the meaning of life. It inspires me even more. Such trips leave an impression not only in the memory of the camera in the form of photographs, but also in the heart in the form of deep emotions and revelations.

I'll tell you the story of the first time I went to the Woodstock rock festival in Poland and visited the Hare Krishna people who were preaching there.

Be it a festival where various subcultures gather, hippies and punks, to seek spiritual truths, or just vagabonds who temporarily brighten up their loneliness with people of a different kind. Diving into history, we find the origins of this legendary party.

In 1969, in America, on the farm of an old man, the first Woodstock took place, which was attended by about 500 thousand people. To raise this grandiose project, world-famous singers were invited. Groups such as Canned Heat and Jefferson Airplane, Janis Joplin, Joan Baez, Joe Cocker, Jimi Hendrix, Ravi Shankar & Carlos Santana.

For some performers, Woodstock became a turning point in their lives and played a very important role in their future careers. Essentially, Woodstock is a rock festival, which, along with heavy music, also included various accompanying elements such as alcohol and drugs, but in addition to everything else, people come here who promote life in harmony with nature, vegetarianism and raw foodism, giving up tobacco and alcohol, and also the use of alternative medicine.

Missionaries of various faiths come here to preach: Catholics, Adventists, Pentecostals, Protestants. The people from Hare Krishna stand out most brightly here; they, more than anyone else, have launched their preaching in the vastness of Woodstock.

Every year, a few weeks before the start of the festival, they build a tent camp called the Peaceful Krishna Village.

They also set up a field kitchen and prepare fresh hot food. People from almost all corners of the festival come here to taste their exquisite dishes. There is already a tradition that the Hare Krishna society is part of Woodstock, and everyone who comes to the festival must traditionally taste their divine food, which is called prasad.

Naturally, I was not left out either, and the first thing I did upon arrival was try the sacred food. After eating a lot, I decided to walk around and see how everything was arranged here. To my surprise, all the places were occupied.

The entire field was filled with tents. They grew like mushrooms after heavy rain, and even people lived near the toilet and the trash, and there was a lot of trash here. On the way, I met various weirdos in unusual clothes.

There are a lot of such guys here. I got the impression that the university is also a space for realizing your creative abilities and the most extraordinary desires. My acquaintance with the local sights was interrupted by singing that came from the shopping arcade, where they sold extravagant clothes and jewelry for non-conformist people.

It was ratha-yatra, a huge procession with songs and dances, in the center of which was a large chariot covered in red fabric and trimmed with gold threads. It splashed and shimmered in the sun, and at the head of this procession was the legendary wandering monk, His Holiness Indradvaya Swami Maharaja.

In his entire life, he traveled the world several times preaching the science of God and even in his old age continues to do so. For many years, he steadily preaches at the festival and, judging by his enthusiasm, he is not going to stop.

Later I learned that as soon as everything began, the organizer of the Woodstock festival personally provided tents and all the necessary equipment for the Hare Krishna people to organize their program here as part of Mustek.

Before this, they had already had a tour of the Baltic Sea and even had their own musical group. They just needed to take and transfer everything to Woodstock. The most amazing thing was that such people like Indradvaya Swami Maharaja devote his whole life to preaching and comes to places where all sorts of human vices live, giving all his time and attention to people living for their own pleasure and using substances that destroy their lives.

And in addition to distributing food, Hare Krishna people carry out preaching activities where they distribute books and everyone can ask questions related to self-awareness.

And I have accumulated a lot of questions: why was I born?

What is the meaning of life?

Why do we get sick?

Why do we suffer?

What is happiness?

Because this is what I live for. But around the chariot, women and men in Indian outfits were dancing and loudly singing Hare Krishna, gradually involving in this hot dance an abundance of drunk punks and rockers who, leaving cans of their beer on the side of the road, merrily dance and indistinctly repeat songs.

And it was a powerful ratha-yatra chariot ride. When I saw it for the first time several years ago, I was very surprised that instead of the buckle itself, there were people pulling on long ropes. This harness weighed more than half a ton and looked like the Trojan Horse from ancient Greek mythology.

Just like in the story with the horse, there were warriors in it, only not a whole army, one person driving the chariot, and at the top stood the deities for whom this whole holiday was arranged. I study Vedic philosophy and learned that these are the deities worshiped throughout the universe and everyone has food: Jagannatha, Baladeva and Subhadra, and they were made from the tree of desire that grows in the spiritual world, and everyone who sees these deities or pulls the chariot by the ropes will open the gates to the world of God.

Hare Krishna used to have a very small village, and Woodstock itself was small, but over time, everything acquired a global scale and many more people began to come, so the Krishna village expanded along with its extraordinary inhabitants.

Krishna consciousness at Woodstock has an influence on all of Poland and even an influence on the entire planet.

People come here not only from all over the country but also from all over the world. The first thing that catches your eye is cleanliness. This is only the external aspect of this culture. Due to the low level of consciousness of people, there is a lot of garbage at the festival, but when they come to relax in the Krishna village and see something pure, they look at people, Hare Krishna eats their frequency of sincerity and thinks, why can't we do that?

And then they leave and do the same at home. Everything happens by comparison. Arriving at Woodstock, people receive their first contact with God through the consciousness of Krishna.

Here they see deities, take sacred food, communicate with preachers, and this moment is very important for their spiritual life. It has enormous consequences. It is very difficult for an ordinary person to come into contact with God, and it is very difficult to lead to this, but here at Woodstock, thousands of people so easily come into contact with spiritual energy, and this first contact means that the soul begins its path to God, and the road to the spiritual world has begun.

It goes. For Indradvaya Swami Maharaja, this is a special place, the best for spreading Krishna consciousness.

Once upon a time, the founder of the Hare Krishna movement, Śrīla Prabhupāda, began preaching in exactly such a place.

Whether he went to universities, or to politics, or to aristocrats who were well-mannered, or no, he went to places where there were drugs, chaos, where people were looking for the purpose of life. It was like an oasis transported by space.

Since the time of Śrīla Prabhupāda, nothing has changed here, it simply manifested itself.

In this place, the university that existed in America in 1969 was no different from today's. The atmosphere was the same as then. People were looking for the purpose of life. They were looking for something new. They needed a revolution.

Just like at the first Woodstock, people used LSD and various drugs and tried to find another state, another world. In cities, most people are focused on money, work, and getting high positions. They come here to break away from this system.

They drink beer and use drugs, but unfortunately, there is no happiness. After another such illusion, they go out into the street and see unusual people, bright and joyful, who are happy without intoxication and other bad habits. People from Hare Krishna, they come to visit them to take prasad, ask questions, and little by little open up like a hedgehog with its soft part of the body.

Of course, not everything will happen at once. Not everyone will immediately become pure, serious people accepting God, because it takes even several lives. But in the perspective of eternity, the fact that we have lived millions and billions of years in this world is only a moment.

Because if Lord Kṛṣṇa sent a person to this place, sent this soul and made him dance, it means God has a plan for this person. Kṛṣṇa wants this particular person to return to Him, and if Kṛṣṇa wants, then it will definitely happen.
