"Today, we arrived in a very special place that is connected with the pastimes of our beloved Lord. Yesterday, we talked about Śrīla Haridāsa Ṭhākura, how he is special, and how dear he is to our International Society for Krishna Consciousness. Śrī Haridāsa Ṭhākura was the one who chanted the holy names, and that is why he is dear to our hearts. Naturally, we talk about him, and we desire to have his mercy, to live in Krishna consciousness, and to have variety. There are many personalities from whom you can choose—friends, mentors, and all the wonderful YouTube dikṣā-gurus. It is an empty stomach; we also have a large variety from which you can choose for yourself a spiritual teacher, a dikṣā-guru, a śikṣā-guru, or even more, as much as you like.

Hare Krishna. Each of us has our own favorite incarnation of the Lord, which is our most beloved. Ultimately, we are all devotees of Krishna. This is what the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam definitively says: $ete cāṃśa-kalāḥ puṃsaḥ kṛṣṇas tu bhagavān svayam$. This means that of all incarnations, full and partial, Krishna is the Supreme Personality of Godhead. He is not just an avatar; he is the source from whom everything comes—the *avatārī*. 

Krishna comes into this world personally, but sometimes he comes in a special mood with a special purpose. For example, He is strong, and He deals with all the demons. The Lord saved the devotees; He is very intelligent and He satisfies everyone. From time to time, as the need arises, a certain incarnation of Krishna appears. It is said that there are more incarnations than can be counted. There are as many incarnations as there are types of life. We know there are incarnations as fish, turtles, and many others. There are also incarnations when He shows His anger toward the lust, anger, and greed within us.

Everything we know about God—His names, forms, pastimes, and paraphernalia—is transcendental. We should not think that what God says or does is subject to material thoughts and emotions. Krishna does not come into contact with material energy; He is above it. Like a king who comes to a prison, he can walk around and check the facility, but he is not a prisoner. When the Lord is angry, we must understand that this is transcendental. A reasonable person sees with his ears, not his eyes. The scriptures explain that this anger is not a reaction to disappointment, like when a person cannot satisfy their material desires. Krishna is forever satisfied and He does not need anything. So, what is the source of the Lord's anger? Mainly, it is when the Lord wants to protect His devotee. When His devotees are in trouble, He becomes angry. For example, when love for Krishna begins to manifest in your heart, if someone begins to revile Krishna, you feel it—your face and hands begin to tremble. Krishna is your beloved, and when someone insults Him, He also begins to feel anger. 

It is like a two-way street. If someone insults a devotee, it is as if they are insulting Krishna. There is a story from my childhood: I once asked my dad where he got a scar on his head. He said, "Ask your mom." And when I asked my mom, she told me the story. When my parents were at university, a group of guys insulted my mother. My father defended her and defeated all of them, but he received a wound on his head with a metal rod. That is a very close analogy; if someone insults a devotee, Krishna is ready to protect him. 

If someone insults Krishna or a devotee, one should either cut out that person's tongue (philosophically, by defeating them in argument) or simply leave if you cannot do anything. If we continue to listen to insults, our consciousness becomes polluted. On the internet, there is a lot of unreliable information. If we read it out of curiosity, doubts begin to creep into the heart, and awareness is defiled. We should not listen to or read any criticism. Sometimes it is necessary to scold these people, but this must come from a very pure person with pure motives, someone who is a very advanced spiritual leader.

When the Lord reviles those who insult His devotees, He begins to show His anger. This transcendental sign shows us how powerful the spiritual love of the Lord is. He loves the devotee so much that even His incarnations act to protect them. Just look at the history of Prahlāda Mahārāja and Hiraṇyakaśipu. 

In the Bible, it is said that the Lord is very merciful and He gave humanity His beloved son, Jesus. When the Lord saved His devotees, He showed that anyone who finds themselves in trouble will be saved by the Lord. When He absorbed their sins, His body turned dark. Whoever grieves a devotee, all those sins will pass on to that person. When God killed Hiraṇyakaśipu, He thereby gave a sign to everyone that He will do the same to anyone who offends His devotee. 

We are all, one might say, trying to become liberated. Our liberation is a matter of time, since we are all standing on this path. We must find that mood of urgency, thinking about those who know nothing about Krishna, who have not tasted *prasāda* or heard the holy names. When Bhaktivinoda Ṭhākura wrote about the relationship of the human race, he noted that everyone is fighting for existence, and the only hope is the Divine Mercy. Krishna says that a devotee should not worry; He will provide everything they need and save what they have. If the Lord cares about everyone—even animals and ants—do you think He will forget the devotees who serve Him? You need to accept everything as His grace, not out of greed, and then Krishna will protect you.

Krishna tells us that there are obstacles that prevent us from finding pure devotion—lust, anger, and greed. They are difficult to get rid of, just like it is difficult to clean a burnt saucepan. But what is difficult becomes easy when you receive the mercy of the Lord. Lord Nṛsiṃhadeva also comes, especially for the purpose of fighting these inner demons. If you pray to this ancient deity, He will help you free yourself from these enemies.

When you repeat the *mantra*, you really need to be sincere. Don't just chant while looking at your phone. When we chant, at the beginning, there will simply be a request for a feeling, and then we can ask for love. Because our hearts are not yet purified, first we must ask for purification: "Oh, Lord, I don’t need honors or wealth. The only thing I want is, please, cleanse my heart."

Everything should happen in its own time. Gradually, the process of purification begins. We ask to be rid of false motives and desires. And sometimes we see that a devotee gets into a difficult situation, but we also know that Krishna knows what our hearts produce. A devotee shows greatness when he remains steady in trouble. Don't be like Kuntī-devī, repeating that "let these misfortunes come again and again"—you don't need to invite them, but you should always pray to be protected from internal enemies. 

Sometimes, under special circumstances, a devotee can ask Krishna to protect him so that he can continue to perform devotional service and give Krishna pleasure. We need to be in good health to preach. I have seen many times how Śrīla Prabhupāda signed his letters: "I hope that this letter finds you in good health." We must remember that we are Krishna's followers, and our goal is Goloka Vṛndāvana.

In conclusion, I will read this poem, which was written by a Vaiṣṇava from Navadvīpa:

*When will that day come, O Lord, when I will sincerely ask for Krishna-prema?*
*My six enemies will leave my heart, and I will be free from hypocrisy and deception.*
*When will I pray for the desire to serve Krishna?*
*When will Lord Nṛsiṃhadeva be pleased and reward me with His mercy?*
*He looks terrifying for those who have an impure mind, but He is very gentle toward devotees like Prahlāda Mahārāja.*
*When will He make this useless fool fearless and engage me in the service of Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa?*
*By the mercy of His devotee, all obstacles will go away from my heart.*

When will Lord Nṛsiṃhadeva pronounce these words and place His lotus feet on my head? Thank you for allowing me to speak before you today. I was in the darkness of material existence, but my spiritual teacher opened my eyes with the torch of knowledge. I am very happy that I came here; this definitely refers to one of my favorite places. I am very lucky to be here with you. Now we will go further and will continue to speak in *hari-nāma*.""
