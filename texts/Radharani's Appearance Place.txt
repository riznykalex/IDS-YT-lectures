"# Rādhārāṇī’s Appearance Place

Good morning, dear devotees. We would like to take this opportunity to welcome you to another very amazing place. Devotees do not often visit this place because it is quite far from Vṛndāvana; you need to go by car to get here. But it is worth it because this is the place where our beloved Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī was born. 

We all know that Kṛṣṇa appeared in Mathurā in the prison of King Kaṁsa, but we do not often hear about the place where Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī appeared. Devotees more often like to go to Varṣāṇā, the place where She spent Her youthful pastimes and lived with Her friends. At that time, Kṛṣṇa lived in Nandagrāma. We talked about these difficulties and mentioned those experienced when She married Āyana and moved to Jāvaṭ-grāma. 

Let me remind you what Yoga-pīṭha is—this is the place where the Lord appeared, the place where His hlādinī-śakti, the energy of pleasure, resides. This place is Rāwal, and Śrī Rūpa Gosvāmī describes this city in a wonderful way. He calls it "Rāwal-āvalī," which translates to "the main of all the cities of Vṛndāvana." When we talk about Vṛndāvana, we mean the village of Vṛndāvana or most of the Vraja-maṇḍala, and in the whole Vraja-maṇḍala, this city is the main one. In one of his prayers, he says: "I pray that at the moment when I enter the capital of Vṛṣabhānu, in which live a large number of gopas and also cows, I will drown in the greatest transcendental bliss and love." 

It is wonderful how he describes this place as one inhabited by gopīs, gopas, and cows, addressing it as the capital of the cities that belong to Vṛṣabhānu. What does he hope for when he comes here to visit? What is he looking for in the flood of transcendental bliss? Thus, we should always follow his desire to drown in the flow of transcendental mercy and love. It lies in the nām-bhajan that we must chant together. Of course, Mahāprabhu’s chanting of the beautiful bhajan helped us in this, and Bihārī will also sing today before we finish the program, because kīrtana is the best and easiest way to attain this mood.

## Discrepancies Regarding the Appearance Place

There are discrepancies among those who have studied where Rādhārāṇī actually appeared. Some claim it is Rāwal, and some say it is Varṣāṇā. Some sages and scholars argue for Varṣāṇā, others for Rāwal. Which opinion shall we accept? There is a third opinion that does not support either the first or the second; they say that in this era She came to Rāwal, and in the previous one, to Varṣāṇā. 

It is interesting how Her arrival is described. In fact, Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī is hidden in the śāstras; She is described by Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam as "Arādhanā." They do not call Her by Her direct name, but they say that it is She who, among other friends of Kṛṣṇa, gives Him the highest pleasure, and thus Her name Rādhārāṇī came about. We know a lot about Kṛṣṇa from His childhood pastimes—how He crawled, what games took place—but there is not much information regarding Rādhārāṇī. It is not written down; it is passed on by word of mouth from grandmother to great-grandmother through the generations. Our people, to some extent, accept this kind of transmission. 

Śarad-vihārī Mahārāja shared with me that there are three stories about how Rādhārāṇī appeared. One explanation is that one day, Her father Vṛṣabhānu was plowing and he raised Her transcendental body from the ground and presented Her to his beloved wife Kīrtidā. This version is very similar to the story of how Sītā Devī appeared when Her father Janaka also plowed the field and found Her in the ground. 

The next version says that Rādhārāṇī was found on a lotus flower. You can imagine a lotus with eight petals, and in the middle, a transcendental Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī was shining. Imagine the beauty of this picture: Vṛṣabhānu sees this beautiful Śrīmatī before him. He takes this baby and hugs Her, and feeling Her touch, he feels transcendental waves of ecstasy in his body. 

Another version says that Rādhārāṇī came from the womb of Her mother, Kīrtidā. In any case, this is a divine phenomenon; Kīrtidā was not pregnant in the usual way from her husband. This was a divine conception. She appeared on the eighth day of the bright moon in the month of Bhādra, which falls in August or September.

## The First Meeting of Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa

We do not have much information about Her childhood games compared to Kṛṣṇa's. People are naturally more favorable to Rādhārāṇī, knowing that the path to Kṛṣṇa lies through Her mercy. One of the most beloved līlās is the moment when Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa first met. One might assume they met as teenagers at some social gathering, but no—they met while still babies. This was organized by the power of Yoga-māyā. 

Everything began at home with the arrival of Nārada Muni. Shortly after Kṛṣṇa appeared and moved in, Nārada wanted to receive His darśana. He knew Kṛṣṇa did not stay long in Mathurā because He was threatened, so he eagerly awaited His arrival at the house of Nanda Bābā and Yaśodā. 

While Nārada Muni received the darśana of Lord Kṛṣṇa, at the behest of Yoga-māyā, he heard a message through his heart that the energy of pleasure of the Lord, hlādinī-śakti Śrīmatī Rādhikā, had appeared nearby. Nārada decided to find Her, traveling around asking local residents about the divine phenomenon. After some time, he arrived at the village where the daughter had appeared to King Vṛṣabhānu. 

A broad smile lit up the King’s face as he said, "By the grace of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, I was given a daughter who possesses all divine qualities." But then he lowered his head and looked sad: "Unfortunately, this divine daughter has not yet opened Her eyes." We think of this as a divine being who does not want to contemplate the imperfections of this material world. They do not want to look at sinful deeds or hear about sins; they prefer to chant the Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra. 

Nārada Muni asked, "Can I see Her?" King Vṛṣabhānu ordered them to bring his daughter. Nārada, being a realized exalted soul, immediately saw this was the Śrīmatī Herself and could not control his ecstasy. Holy people at a high level of development usually restrain their emotions to avoid putting them on display, but sometimes these emotions manifest. Just as Jesus Christ spoke of his cup overflowing with love, Nārada Muni, at the moment he received the darśana of the baby, could not contain his ecstatic emotions; they overwhelmed him, and he began to sweat and shed tears, his voice breaking. 

King Vṛṣabhānu asked him, "I understand this is a divine blessing, but Her eyes are closed. You are a great holy man; could you please help us?" This is the qualification of holy people: to give blessings and help withstand material difficulties. While Vṛṣabhānu went to collect the ingredients for a special auspicious ceremony, Nārada was left alone with the girl. Knowing who was in front of him, he clung to Her and whispered many beautiful prayers. 

As Nārada offered his prayers, Rādhārāṇī fidgeted in Her cradle. She knew the Lord knows the hearts of His devotees, and She showed him Her real form as a young girl of eight or nine years old. Nārada wanted to glorify Her, but he was frozen like stone from contemplating such a divine darśana. 

Unexpectedly, King Vṛṣabhānu entered the room, and Rādhārāṇī immediately took the form of a baby again. Nārada was still frozen and deprived of speech. King Vṛṣabhānu asked, "Is everything fine with you?" Nārada finally performed the ceremony he intended. After that, he departed with an even greater desire to serve Her. 

### Seeking Service
That brief moment of darśana changed his life; he was then completely concentrated on the desire to receive Her association or service. We are currently trying to get this service again through the association of devotees, kīrtana, prasādam, and chanting the Hare Kṛṣṇa mantra, which is a request: "O Lord, please engage me in serving You." As Mahātma Prabhu said, this chanting should not be mechanical; it should come from your heart. 

Nārada Muni could not forget this darśana; he went to Vārṣāṇā and settled there, performing austerities. This place is now called Nārada-vana. Thanks to these austerities, he was able to enter the līlā of Kṛṣṇa to perform service. 

Even while preaching and traveling, he never forgets the lotus feet of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī. There is a prayer recorded in Gāndharva-Saṁhitā that says: "I travel, therefore, on this planet to meditate on Her who, like the Moon, illuminates the dance, the one who is the abode of all the sparkling lamps in the palace of King Vṛṣabhānu, and the one who is the central jewel in the decoration around Vraja-loka."

## The Conclusion of the Divine Play
King Vṛṣabhānu was not disappointed that Her eyes remained closed after Nārada’s visit, as he had clear faith that such a high-level sadhu’s presence would be favorable. The next day, Yoga-māyā (Paurṇamāsī) came to the house. She said, "This is an extraordinary girl. Such exalted personalities as Brahmā and Nārada worship Her." 

Later, Nanda Mahārāja came with little Kṛṣṇa. As they gathered in the hallway to talk, little Kṛṣṇa, who knows everything, crawled toward the room where Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī lay. He made His way to the manger, looked in, and immediately understood that this was His eternal divine beloved. With His little hands, He reached out and gently touched Her eyes. 

The moment Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī felt the touch of His hands, She realized it was the person for whom Her eyes were created. Overcoming Her blindness, She opened Her shining blue eyes. This was how They first met. When the parents finally discovered Them, they saw little Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa playing together. Vṛṣabhānu and Kīrtidā were ecstatic to find the eyes of their daughter were open—a beautiful blue color—and they organized a magnificent festival. 

Everything happened as it was meant to be. We are now in the best place, and there is no need to wander anywhere else. Let us stay here and listen to the pastimes that happened. We will gather again at 10:30 to listen to more līlā, and then Bīhārī will finish with a wonderful number. Please fully enjoy these last moments of our Parikrama 2016.""
