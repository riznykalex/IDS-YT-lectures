"High in the Himalayas lies the source of one of India's holiest rivers, the sacred Yamunā. 

Known as Yamunotrī, it is just one of many holy places located in the northern state of Uttarakhanda. Because of there being so many sacred places here, this area is sometimes called Deva-bhūmi, or literally, "land of the gods." Since the beginning of time, great sages and mystics have come to this sacred area to perform penances, austerities, and worship of the Lord, with the goal of purifying all material desires and ultimately awakening love of God. 

Even among these special holy places, the source of the sacred River Yamunā is considered especially potent. 

The ancient scriptures of India, known as the Vedas, explain that just as different incarnations of the Lord come from the spiritual world to bring benefit to all living beings, similarly, the holy rivers also come down from the spiritual reality. In this way, the source of the Yamunā River is the spiritual world, from where she flows down to Śrī Vṛndāvana-dhāma here in India. 

By taking a bath in holy rivers such as Mother Ganges, one can be relieved of all negative karma from previous lives, and these material desires, which have kept us bound up life after life, are thrown to a distant place. However, the great teachers of the sacred bhakti-yoga tradition explain that the waters of Śrī Yamunā Devī are even more special than those of the sacred Ganges, because by bathing in her waters, one can not only be freed from all sinful reactions but can develop pure love for the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Śrī Kṛṣṇa, and His eternal consort.""
