Regarding riddles, they are actually an ancient art form blending logic, wit, and poetic rhythm in India. Many ācāryas state that Vedic wisdom was originally delivered in riddles before Śrīla Vyāsadeva compiled and simplified it.

They say that Lord Brahmā encoded the Vedas in riddle form to conceal their true meaning from the demoniac.

They expressed it in this way:

“Since the unwise cannot unravel riddles, sacred truths remain hidden from those who might misuse them.”

Now, in ancient Sanskrit, this hidden form of wisdom was called *kīlaka*, meaning “to lock” or “to nail something.”

Each *sūkta*, each hymn, was said to be locked by such riddling nails, and only a bona fide guru could unlock them by his spiritual potency, *bala*, revealing their true meaning to his disciples.

Later, out of compassion, Vyāsadeva revealed their essence for the less intelligent souls of Kali-yuga.
