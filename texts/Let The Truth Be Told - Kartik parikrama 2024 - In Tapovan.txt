From the very moment He dragged them along, the boys were like, "Kāṇai, where are we going? It's early, so where are You dragging us?" They were 4–5 years old, and Śrīla Prabhupāda says that because they were so young, their presence there did not interfere in any way. Since they were children, everyone treated them leniently. If a dog came and did something here, everyone would start yelling, and if a child did something, everyone would say, "Well, what should you do, child, just get rid of it." Such a lenient attitude was towards children. Since they were very young, their presence was not an insult. 

"Seek and you will find." Rūpa Gosvāmī said that the boys were actually 2–3 years old, and Śrīla Prabhupāda comments and says that Kṛṣṇa made sure that He was accompanied by very young friends, because if older boys had gone, it would have caused discomfort. At the moment when Kṛṣṇa came there, He tucked His black hair into a turban, raised His *dhotī* above His knees higher, and also took off His ankle jewelry. Then very carefully, on tiptoe, they approached the clothes of the gopīs which were lying on the shore. Then there was a description that Kṛṣṇa very elegantly threw the gopīs' clothes over His shoulder and He climbed the Kadamba tree, which was nearby on the bank of the Yamunā River where the girls were washing themselves. Since the boys were not accustomed to girls' clothes, it was very disturbing for them; they spoke quietly because they were laughing. Then Kṛṣṇa, very menacingly addressing the gopīs, spoke to them and said, "About the gopīs whom you insulted because you bathe naked in the water, I sent My servants so that your father would take your clothes! And look, even the Goddess is very unhappy with you because she did not keep clothes for you!" 

Hearing these words, the gopīs replied—do you want to know what the gopīs said? Śyāmasundara said, "Our bodies are trembling from the cold water, why are You so cruel to us and mocking us? We understand perfectly well that You did all this and only You stole our clothes. Please kindly return our clothes to us because we need to get out of this icy water." Remember that all this is happening on a transcendental platform. Kṛṣṇa answers, "Well, if these clothes are really yours, then come out and take them from me." Ācāryas write that the gopīs, seeing that Kṛṣṇa was full of determination, made their hearts overflow with the greatest ecstasy. Of course, they were very embarrassed. On the other hand, they really liked this attention from Kṛṣṇa directed at them, precisely the fact that He joked with them as with His girlfriends or even as with His wives, because the Vedic tradition is that a woman never appears naked in front of anyone except her husband. 

In fact, ācāryas write that Paramātmā organized this game so that Kṛṣṇa would accept these gopīs as His wives—everything according to the rules. Well, of course, this would never have happened; they could never have become Kṛṣṇa's wives. Why? Because the main *bhāva*, or mood of *rasa* in Vṛndāvana, is *parakīyā-bhāva*—the love of unmarried people. This is a very deep topic. If they actually married Kṛṣṇa, then the sublime *prakīya-rasa* would disappear and it would no longer be the mood of Vṛndāvana. On a transcendental platform, *para-rasa* means everything for the satisfaction of Kṛṣṇa. *Para-rasa* is more sublime than the love between officially married people. The gopīs and Kṛṣṇa played with each other in this way, but there was never a wedding; it was all a game. This is the village of Lalitā-sakhī; it is in this place that Lalitā marries Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa together. It is all a game, actually, because after they play this, they go to their homes, and there are also ceremonies in which even the gopīs marry Kṛṣṇa. These are very deep themes. I remember that I was somewhere just at that time. The topics are very deep, so the temple president tried for a long time to find someone who would dare to talk about these topics, but this is present in *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam*. 

We must treat this correctly. When the gopīs saw that Kṛṣṇa was determined, they realized that they would have no other option. One gopī, turning to another, said, "Dear friend, it seems Kṛṣṇa has destroyed all our arguments, and our fate is inevitable, so let's answer. Let's just close our eyes to Kṛṣṇa's request and throw aside any shame and get out of the water." Śrīla Prabhupāda says that Kṛṣṇa, as a satisfied person, had no interest in seeing girls naked; rather, His interest was teaching the world through these gopīs that religion means complete self-surrender. Whatever I do, everything for His satisfaction and nothing for me. Let's remember that *līlās* are devoid of any lust. We have material lust, they have transcendental bodies that are created from the eternity of knowledge and bliss; therefore, no lust is characteristic of them. Prabhupāda said that lust is what you want to get for yourself, and love is what you want to give to Kṛṣṇa. 

Kṛṣṇa was delighted with their devotion and therefore gave them back the clothes. In fact, He responded to the level of surrender and love that they had because Kṛṣṇa can be controlled by the love of devotees for Him. In *Govinda-līlāmṛta*, there is a description of how love controls Kṛṣṇa: the moon is naturally warm, the earth is naturally cold, the wind is always restless, saints are always very serious, and the ocean is naturally deep; in the same way, Kṛṣṇa is controlled by the love of His devotees. Let's listen how Śrīla Prabhupāda commented on this game. The gopīs are very simple in nature, so everything that Kṛṣṇa tells them, they do, in order to complete their vows addressed to the Kātyāyanī and also in order to fulfill what Kṛṣṇa told them. They just took and came out of the water, and thus they became the greatest lovers of Kṛṣṇa because they were the most obedient and His servants. Nothing can compare with the consciousness of Kṛṣṇa that He had. 

In fact, the gopīs are not interested in any demigods; the only thing they want is to satisfy Kṛṣṇa. So this game was needed in order to manifest and show how they are completely devoted to Kṛṣṇa. Ācāryas continue, and thank God that he writes all this, after the gopīs put their clothes back on, they stood there and looked embarrassedly, looked towards their beloved Kṛṣṇa. Then he writes something wonderful; they could not leave this *līlā* because Kṛṣṇa did not steal their clothes—in fact, He stole their hearts. In conclusion, he writes that stealing the clothes was only an excuse to become a means by which they can show their complete devotion to Him and develop their love for Him. We must understand, in the end, all the austerities that the gopīs performed for Him were rewarded, and the pain they experienced during the austerities was rewarded by the fact that Kṛṣṇa stood next to them. In their hearts, he writes that if someone is dear to you, then everything He does gives you great joy, even if it is associated with pain. None of the girls married Kṛṣṇa; it was all a game. But because the unmarried relationship between Kṛṣṇa and His gopīs has deeper tastes, they showed that they are actually the most selfless devotees. So we worship these. 

Mahāprabhu writes, "I pray that this cowherd boy who casts loving glances from the corner of His restless eyes, whose cheeks are adorned with radiance and also adorned with sparkling earrings shaped like a shark, who loves to enjoy the *rāsa* dance, who attracts beautiful girls in Vraja, may He one day manifest in my heart." Rūpa Gosvāmī, in *Vraja-līlā-stava*, describing this *līlā*, writes: "They entered the waters of Yamunā preparing to worship the goddess Kātyāyanī, these girls of Vraja left their clothes on the shore. Smiling, very, very smart Kṛṣṇa, He stole these clothes, climbed the Kadamba tree, confused these girls with His jester words. I take shelter of this *līlā-ghāṭa*; this is the place where the Lord enjoyed His games." I understand that it was a very intimate, deep, deep lecture. Perhaps it was beyond our understanding, but the truth had to be told. This game had its place; it is described in *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam* and also in *Kṛṣṇa, the Source of Eternal Pleasure*. It was commented on by those who tell this as we know it ourselves, and it really helps our development of Kṛṣṇa consciousness to hear about the selfless love of the gopīs and their complete surrender to Him. Since we are in Gopī-ghāṭa in Tapovana, I could not give any other lecture about the fact that "you are not this body, you are the soul." I had to tell about the game that happened here, and thank you for listening.""
