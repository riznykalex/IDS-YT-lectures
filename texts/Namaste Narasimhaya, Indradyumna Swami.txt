"So, in mid-to-late 1995, I participated in the opening of the Udaypur temple. Many influential people participated in the opening of the temple. The state governor arrived, and besides, among the invited guests, there was also a local devotee, a very elderly Vaiṣṇava with gray hair and a large tilaka of Nṛsiṁhadeva. He came to the opening; he supervised nine temples that were located there. After the installation, you perform the installation ceremony, and after the opening ceremony, the devotees sang the Hare Kṛṣṇa mahā-mantra. 

It was the first time I heard the devotees sing a prayer to the Lord, and suddenly, this venerable leader of the temples of Nṛsiṁhadeva began to roar like a lion. His guide, Śrīla Bhakti Purusottama Swami, said he imitated the roar of a lion so well that I won’t even risk reproducing it. And he also made such dramatic movements with his hands, like he was tearing someone apart. We looked at him with disbelief because, truly, he warned us that he was in trance and did not show signs of ecstasy in public, so we all looked at him. 

He was immersed in the mood of Lord Nṛsiṁhadeva. It was not that he was merely imitating; he was so immersed in his consciousness of the Lord that similar emotions manifested in him.""
