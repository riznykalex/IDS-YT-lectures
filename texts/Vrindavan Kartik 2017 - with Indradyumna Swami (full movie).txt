### Visit to the Āśrama of Durvāsā Muni

Our place of visitation today—the wonderful thing about going to the āśrama of Durvāsā Muni is that we always go up the Yamunā by boat. This boat ride to Durvāsā Muni’s āśrama is one of the highlights of our Kārttika parikrama. It is a nice opportunity for the devotees to get away from the sounds of car horns and the rickshaws always in the path, and to be on the Yamunā Devī herself, appreciating the wonderful pastimes that Kṛṣṇa enacted here over 5,000 years ago. Reaching our hands into the sacred Yamunā water, we are able to take that water and sprinkle it on our heads and purify ourselves as we approach the āśrama of Durvāsā Muni. 

It is about a one-hour ride in the boat down the Yamunā, during which one gets to see many of the different pilgrimage places, which we enjoy very much. Arriving at the āśrama of Durvāsā Muni, it is very pleasantly situated on the cliffs overlooking the Yamunā River as it flows down towards Vṛndāvana. Standing on these cliffs amongst the banyan trees and the shade trees of Durvāsā’s āśrama, one gets a feeling for why Durvāsā would have picked such a place as this to execute his pastimes and austerities. It is a very, very wonderful, quiet, peaceful place where one can enjoy the old style of what Vṛndāvana was like many, many years ago, and in some cases, hundreds of years ago.

There is a very fierce deity of Durvāsā Muni with fire-like eyes because he is one that is not to be crossed. There are many pastimes of Durvāsā Muni’s kindness, such as when he gave Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī the benediction that everything she cooked would be just like nectar. And there are many opportunities to read about Durvāsā Muni’s cursing, such as when he inappropriately cursed Ambarīṣa Mahārāja for nothing more than taking a little bit of water. For this act, of course, we know from Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam he was chased throughout the entire three planetary systems by none other than the Sudarśana-cakra of Lord Viṣṇu Himself. Running to this personality and that personality begging for mercy, even to Lord Viṣṇu Himself, Lord Viṣṇu reminded him, "You cannot get My mercy and forgiveness because you have offended My devotee. Therefore, if you want the forgiveness, you have to go to the devotee you have offended." In this way, Durvāsā Muni’s pastimes are not only a direct example of how not to offend devotees, but a direct example of how to make up for such an offense by going to that devotee and begging for their mercy.

So these pastimes we very much relish speaking about. At the āśrama of Durvāsā Muni, there is one banyan tree there which is said to be many thousands of years old, and in that banyan tree are many beautiful parrots that follow in the footsteps of Śuka and Sārī and speak about the pastimes of Lord Kṛṣṇa. Also at that spot are some very, very big monkeys, but these monkeys are quite mellow and they don’t bother most of the devotees there. They like to languish in the tree branches above the places where we speak and hear these pastimes. So for the devotees, this is one of our favorite places and it is very, very much shaded, so even if it is a hot Vṛndāvana day, you don’t feel any distress. And if you feel too much distress, there’s always the opportunity to just jump into the Yamunā and cool oneself off. So in this way, we very much appreciate coming to the āśrama of Durvāsā Muni, speaking about his pastimes, and remembering the activities of all the personalities of Vṛndāvana. In this way, we make our perfect time here at the Kārttika parikrama 2017. These are the reasons that on our parikrama we go to all these various holy places—not simply as a tourist for the sake of going to this place and that place, but while at those places, to take the dust of that place on our head and hear the pastimes from the saintly persons that are with us. In this way, we perfect our life, according to Prabhupāda’s instruction that we should come to these places once a year and purify ourselves. Thank you very much. All glories to Śrīla Prabhupāda. All glories to Vṛndā Devī.

### The City of Jaipur

All right, Kṛṣṇa, and welcome to Jaipur. The city of Jaipur, in the state of Rajasthan, India, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in India, known as the "Pink City" for the centuries-old saffron-pink walls that extend around much of the city. It is rich in the cultural heritage of India. Most important for devotees of the Lord is the presence of a number of Vṛndāvana’s original deities, including Rādhā-Govindajī, Rādhā-Gopīnātha, and Rādhā-Vinoda. Hundreds of years ago, the sacred village of Vṛndāvana was attacked during a foreign invasion, and devotees hid these and other deities from the invaders in the forests and lakes around Vṛndāvana. 

In time, the precarious situation of the deities came to the attention of a powerful and pious Rajput king, Jai Singh II, who eventually built the city of Jaipur as a haven to protect the deities. In fact, he designed the city in such a way that Rādhā-Govindajī’s temple is situated in the exact center of the city. He further designed his own nearby residence in such a way as to be able to directly see the Lord from his palace rooms each day. Morning and evening, thousands of devotees come for darśana of Rādhā and Govindajī; their devoted singing and prayers to these deities of Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī create a spiritual atmosphere that overwhelms the hearts of anyone and everyone present.

Our parikrama party visited the Rādhā-Govindajī temple on several occasions. We also had darśana of Gopīnātha, who were discovered by Madhu Paṇḍita, a close associate of Lord Caitanya, 500 years ago in Vṛndāvana. The unique feature of the Rādhā-Gopīnātha temple is the beautiful bhajanas sung by an enthusiastic group of ladies each morning. The girls in our parikrama party enjoyed singing along with them, as well as dancing in the traditional way the local ladies do. During our visit, we also visited the deities of Lokanātha Gosvāmī, Rādhā-Vinoda, as well as Bhūgarbha Gosvāmī’s famous Rādhā-Dāmodara deities. I used to wonder how it was that all these deities were not returned to Vṛndāvana, but on this visit, I finally understood why they remain in Jaipur to this very day. The answer is that although Kṛṣṇa is all-powerful, He is controlled by the love of His devotees. The devotees of Govindajī, Gopīnātha, Dāmodara, and Rādhā-Vinoda show so much love and affection for these deities that it is impossible for the deities to ever leave. It is something that must be witnessed to believe. I pray this video will reveal to the viewers not only the beautiful historical culture of Jaipur but the deep devotion of its citizens for their beloved Lordships as well. Because devotion for Kṛṣṇa is the essence of Kṛṣṇa consciousness, we can learn much from the Lord’s devotees in Jaipur. Hare Kṛṣṇa.

### Rādhāṣṭamī at Rāval

It is our good fortune that on this auspicious day of Rādhāṣṭamī, the divine appearance of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, we find ourselves in Rāval here in Vṛndāvana with a small group of devotees doing parikrama. Rāval is actually where Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī made her appearance. Later on, she transferred with her family over to Varṣāṇā, just as Kṛṣṇa appeared in Gokula and later transferred over to Nandagrāma with His family. So this is a very, very special place. Today here in Vṛndāvana, you could say this is probably the biggest day of the year for the Brajavāsīs; so many Brajavāsīs have come here to Rāval, and from here they go to Varṣāṇā. So we are following in the footsteps of the Brajavāsīs. 

In the Garga-saṁhitā, it is described that when it was time for Lord Kṛṣṇa to make His appearance at the end of the Dvāpara-yuga as the Yuga-avatāra, He approached His divine consort, Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, and requested her to come with Him to the earth planet to perform their pastimes to attract the conditioned souls back home, back to Godhead. Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī agreed, but she made a condition that she would only come if they were accompanied by three things: the forests of Vṛndāvana, Govardhana Hill, and the River Yamunā. Kṛṣṇa agreed, and Rādhārāṇī agreed to come. So it is by the mercy of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī that Vṛndāvana-dhāma has manifested here in the material world. In the spiritual world, it is called Goloka Vṛndāvana, and when that Goloka Vṛndāvana comes to the material plane, it is called Gokula Vṛndāvana. Actually, there is no difference; the ācāryas say there is no difference between Goloka and Gokula, and those whose eyes are purified by devotion, they can see the full glory of Vṛndāvana manifest in the material world here in Vṛndāvana.

We have come here, and as all Gauḍīya Vaiṣṇavas know, Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī is the best devotee of Kṛṣṇa. She has the mahābhāva. We are all aspiring for bhāva, or for devotion to the divine couple, but Rādhārāṇī, Mādhavendra Purī, and Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu are the three personalities mentioned in scripture who have that mahābhāva. She made her appearance here in Rāval. Just behind me, you can see a kuṇḍa—actually, it is nothing more than a kuṇḍa, but previously, this was where the River Yamunā flowed. One day, her father, King Vṛṣabhānu, was near their dwelling here, and King Vṛṣabhānu was walking and he found Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī as a small baby floating on a golden lotus. This is how she made her appearance. Just like Kṛṣṇa—He is not born into this world, we don't say He is born, He makes a divine appearance—in the same way, Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, her pastime was to make her appearance as a small child floating on a golden lotus down the Yamunā River. So he picked her up and brought her here, and this was the beginning of her childhood pastimes. 

Actually, there is not so much said about Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī’s childhood pastimes in the Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam, and our ācāryas explain that is because Śukadeva Gosvāmī, the speaker of the Bhāgavatam, his iṣṭa-deva, his worshipable personality, was Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī. If he would speak so much about her, then he would begin to cry and he wouldn't be able to finish the Bhāgavatam. So Śukadeva Gosvāmī is not speaking so much about Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī. So all these wonderful pastimes took place here. As usual, we engage in the process of hearing and chanting—especially chanting this mahā-mantra—and this enables us to in some way feel and become purified, and enter into these wonderful pastimes that took place here. So we are very grateful today to be at Tāra-kadamba and Yāvaṭa. Hope you enjoy this video and can also feel some of the mood of this place. Thank you, Hare Kṛṣṇa.
