"Namaste and Hare Kṛṣṇa. In a few months' time, from March 1st to March 15th, there is going to be a special spiritual event happening here in India in the sacred town of Rishikesh, at the foothills of the Himalayas. Tens of thousands of young people from all over the world are going to come to Rishikesh to learn about yoga. I would like to be there with another little festival by the side called Kīrtan Fest, and I am appealing to all of you for donations to help make our festival successful.

We plan to rent an ashram near the main event, and during the day, we will go out and sing Hare Kṛṣṇa on the streets. This is the process for this age: chanting the holy names. We will distribute invitations, inviting the people to come back in the evening for a discourse on Bhagavad-gītā—the eternal wisdom of Bhagavad-gītā, which can solve the problems we have even in modern society—for hours of kīrtana and a sumptuous feast.

I will be taking 50 devotees with me. We will be living there for this, maybe even three weeks if some of those festival seekers hang on, introducing them to the original culture of India, which helps us to awaken our dormant love for God, thereby achieving liberation from this world and going back to the spiritual sky. 

So please, this is the culture, the original spiritual culture of the world. People are seeking it, and we are going to present it in this wonderful form of singing, dancing, and feasting. Please give liberally. This is spiritual welfare. We generally give for material welfare, but that is temporary; the problems come again. If we can solve the problems, give a final solution to the problem by going back to the spiritual world, that is spiritual welfare. Please help us facilitate the liberation of these wonderful people coming from all around the world. Kṛṣṇa.""
