"A couple of days ago, your lecture on freedom, in which you said that it is impossible to understand Kṛṣṇa without chanting the holy name of Kṛṣṇa, was wonderful. Personally, when the name is chanted, even without love, you have time to be called here. Yes, of course, it was touching to watch, as we know, Kṛṣṇa is our friend who extracts us from this material city of tanks, and if we sincerely and honestly chant, then Kṛṣṇa will manifest. 

Our main need in life is to sincerely chant and pray, to call upon Kṛṣṇa, and then love Kṛṣṇa. Śrīla Prabhupāda said that chanting the holy name of Kṛṣṇa is our main duty. An engineer filled with nectar of devotion says that we chant the holy name in order to cleanse our hearts. Therefore, you firmly believe this, and once again I want to thank you for this communication. It is described that great thinkers say there is no difference between chanting the holy name and saṅkīrtana; it is directly considered the same thing. Someone who practices Harināma-saṅkīrtana very quickly awakens their dormant love for Kṛṣṇa.

If we communicate with sincere people who have faith in Kṛṣṇa and chant with them, and engage in service with them, the process becomes very easy. Why did you come? The Hare Kṛṣṇa movement is to become Kṛṣṇa conscious. Stop struggling, agree, and surrender. It is so easy to become conscious of Kṛṣṇa. There is no difficulty; you can simply chant this name, communicate with devotees, read books, and engage in service. 

It is difficult because we have a layer of material contamination. If you do not want this layer, then it is not easy to warm up. But if you chant the holy name striving for Kṛṣṇa, what is the difficulty? We must develop this process. Sometimes it seems to us that in the real world everything is hopeless and problems become insurmountable, but we should have complete faith. He is present in the holy name. We should chant with a feeling of helplessness, like a child calling upon his mother.

It was a wonderful discussion. I especially liked the last point, which showed that we are blessed with the opportunity to chant the holy name in communication with each other. This is our strength, and we must not forget that it is in society that we find support. As they say, many hands make any business easier. If one twig can break, if everything is connected together, the structure becomes very strong. 

Kṛṣṇa says that His material energy is very difficult to overcome, but for the one who surrenders to Him, it becomes easy. Therefore, Kṛṣṇa consciousness always gives hope. The best method of chanting is in the association of devotees. 

Once in 1975, we gathered here, and you preached in the mornings and evenings. There was a devotee from New York, and you were close friends. When he arrived, the next day he disappeared. I began to ask about him, and it turned out he was not chanting his sixteen rounds. We cannot condemn anyone, but this is a profound science of chanting the holy name, so it is very important to practice correctly. The best way is to chant in the association of others.

My spiritual brother received this amazing opportunity to chant the holy name next to Śrīla Prabhupāda for many years. One day, Śrīla Prabhupāda asked him, "What are you doing?" He said, "I am repeating, Śrīla Prabhupāda." Prabhupāda asked, "Why? Everything is on the tape." In spiritual life, it is very important to have settings, like tuning an engine so the entire machine works correctly. 

Regarding the result of chanting with offenses: the ācāryas say that if during the chanting of the holy name we do not get tears in our eyes, this means our offenses are still accumulating. Although we are gradually getting rid of them, we have not achieved perfection, so we need to choose the main and most important thing for us, which is the chanting of the Hare Kṛṣṇa mantra. The best time for chanting is early morning.

I remember when I was a teenager, my voice was breaking and I couldn't sing well. I was so disappointed, but one fine evening I began to pray to be given the opportunity to sing. It was a competition for schools throughout Northern California. I was so afraid that I would sing incorrectly, so in order not to risk it when it was necessary to sing high notes, I simply opened my mouth. Everything went well, and I received participation honors. This is not the most perfect analogy, because Kṛṣṇa consciousness does not depend on external factors. The most important thing is how sincerely we serve Kṛṣṇa. God accepts sincerity.

If we alone try to express our devotion, Kṛṣṇa may not even notice, but if we chant in the company of devotees, there are so many people singing there that it creates a powerful vibration. It is impossible to find a better place for chanting the holy name together. I take special advantage of my visits to Māyāpur. Usually, children are simply unable to sit quietly during a lecture, but here in Māyāpur, children sit attentively. This is a natural way of life for them; it is a habit, and habits are very difficult to change. 

I also see how the children here have such strong faith. It is completely untypical for children to sit like this and listen to philosophical lectures for a long time. This is the most important quality—that they have communication with the spiritual teacher and take prasādam together. This is very important. When during the kīrtana I looked at the children, they chanted with such taste. It is wonderful that children have such deep faith and deep immersion.""
