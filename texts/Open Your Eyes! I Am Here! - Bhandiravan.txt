"# Open Your Eyes! I Am Here! - Bhaṇḍīravana

Hare Kṛṣṇa, dear devotees, welcome back. Today, we are going to a unique place, the Bhaṇḍīravana forest. It is one of the 12 main forests of Vṛndāvana. What is the unique feature of this forest? It is described as a favorite place for games, both for the cowherd boys and for various pastimes of the Lord. It also attracts yogīs because of its special spiritual atmosphere. This place is described in the *Bhakti-ratnākara* and by Śrīla Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī, specifically in *Vraja-vilāsa-stava* (text 74), where he glorifies Bhaṇḍīravana.

### The Glory of Bhaṇḍīravana
I offer my obeisances to the birthplace of the pastimes of the 24 avatāras; I offer my obeisances to the original source of various objects associated with serving the Lord, named Bhaṇḍīravana. 

Kṛṣṇa is called Bhaṇḍīrava-nātha, or the Lord of the forest, or the one who dances in this forest. In the *Mathurā Māhātmya* (3.34), it is said that a person who visits this forest will never have to return to the womb of the mother—meaning they will never have to be born or die again. Furthermore, the *Mathurā Māhātmya* (3.36) states that a person with self-control who fasts and takes ablution in the *kūpa* (holy well) there will be freed from all sins and reach the richest abode of the Supreme Lord. Ācāryas explain that "Indra-loka" here does not refer to the planet of the demigod Indra, but rather to the supreme, wealthiest abode of the Supreme Lord, Kṛṣṇa.

### Spiritual Significance and Pastimes
Even if you perform *parikramā* around a single tree in Vraja, you receive great benefit. Śrīla Sanātana Gosvāmī and other ācāryas wandered through all 12 forests to follow in the footsteps of Lord Caitanya, who searched for these places of Kṛṣṇa’s pastimes.

*   **The Talking Parrots:** Śrīdāmā was once surprised to hear two parrots discussing the *Vedānta-sūtra*. This demonstrates that all inhabitants of Vṛndāvana, even birds and trees, are fully Kṛṣṇa-conscious.
*   **Rati Mañjarī’s Painting:** Rati Mañjarī painted a picture of Kṛṣṇa and showed it to Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī. When Kṛṣṇa arrived, He greatly appreciated this painting.
*   **The Wedding līlā:** It is described in scriptures that Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa played out a wedding *līlā* in Bhaṇḍīravana. While they are eternally united, this *līlā* highlights the intensity of their loving relationship. During this event, Nanda Mahārāja received a blessing from Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī: he asked for firm devotion to Her and Kṛṣṇa, and to always live in the company of Their pure devotees.
*   **Radha-Bhaṇḍīravihārī:** There is a beautiful temple where the Deities are in a unique pose: Kṛṣṇa stands on tiptoe, raising His left hand above His head to apply *sindūra* on Rādhārāṇī’s hair parting.

### The Bhaṇḍīra-vaṭa Tree and Venu-kūpa
The Bhaṇḍīra-vaṭa is an ancient banyan tree. It is said that 5,000 years ago, it was so massive that its branches stretched across the Yamunā River, allowing the cowherd boys to cross from one bank to the other.

Next to the tree is the *Veṇu-kūpa*. When a demon named Vatsāsura appeared in the form of a calf, Kṛṣṇa killed him. However, the Gopīs accused Kṛṣṇa of killing a calf and insisted he atone for the sin. Kṛṣṇa poked the earth with His flute, and all the sacred rivers appeared, forming this lake.

### The Friendship of Kṛṣṇa and Śrīdāmā
A famous pastime involves a wrestling game where Kṛṣṇa’s team lost, and the boys teased Kṛṣṇa, saying He was too weak to carry them on His shoulders. Eventually, Śrīdāmā challenged Kṛṣṇa, and Kṛṣṇa carried him. 

Later, when Kṛṣṇa went to Mathurā, Śrīdāmā was devastated by separation. He returned to Bhaṇḍīravana and performed severe penance, refusing to eat or drink, waiting for Kṛṣṇa’s return. When Kṛṣṇa finally returned and found Śrīdāmā in a frail state, He whispered, "Open your eyes, I am here." Seeing Kṛṣṇa, Śrīdāmā was overwhelmed, and the trees—which had stood withered in Kṛṣṇa's absence—suddenly blossomed and showered them with flowers.

### Conclusion
As Raghunātha dāsa Gosvāmī prays: 
> "I glorify Kṛṣṇa’s friend, Śrīdāmā, the most experienced and intelligent of the cowherd boys. He is the abode of the strongest love for Kṛṣṇa... I completely take shelter of Śrīdāmā, the constant companion of the Lord."

By hearing these pastimes, we can cleanse our hearts and increase our attachment to Kṛṣṇa. We will continue our discussion of the 12 forests of Vṛndāvana next Friday. Hare Kṛṣṇa.""
