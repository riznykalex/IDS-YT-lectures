"Regarding the worship of Lord Nṛsiṁhadeva, ancient Vedic kings would install deities of Nṛsiṁhadeva on the borders of their kingdoms so that He would protect the kingdom from enemies. I remember how one of the sannyāsīs during the time of Śrīla Prabhupāda discovered the deities of Lakṣmī-Nṛsiṁhadeva and came to Prabhupāda to ask for his permission to worship Them. Prabhupāda said, "No, no need. You are a sannyāsī; your main duty is preaching. Worshiping the deities will distract you."

Gurukṛpa began to persuade Prabhupāda, "I want to worship Him. Such an amazing Deity!" Prabhupāda replied, "You need to understand that He is always angry, and only the devotion of Prahlāda Mahārāja can calm Him down. You do not have such devotion, so it is not recommended for you to worship Him. You know what happens if you are near someone who is constantly angry; it is very easy to commit an offense. If you commit an offense to the Deity, it can cause catastrophic consequences."

Gurukṛpa was very upset, but Śrīla Prabhupāda showed him mercy and allowed him to worship Nṛsiṁhadeva only with Lakṣmī-devī, since Lakṣmī can neutralize offenses. Śrīla Prabhupāda found a transcendental compromise because he had extensive experience and an understanding of how to properly organize deity worship. The Mahārāja worshipped those Deities for many years, and we all felt protected."
