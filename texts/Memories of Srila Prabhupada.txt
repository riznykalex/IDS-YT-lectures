"# Memories of Śrīla Prabhupāda

I first saw Śrīla Prabhupāda when he came to Detroit in 1971. He visited our church, and we threw a huge, lavish reception at the airport. There were many devotees there. They came from all over: from Chicago, from New York, from St. Louis. Some devotees even came from California. Prabhupāda had not come for a long time, and many of us had never seen him. When he arrived at the airport, it was a deafening kīrtana. Kīrtanānanda Mahārāja tells about this disappearance very vividly and colorfully. 

I remember Prabhupāda got off the plane, and he was walking through the airport in the distance. We held a deafening kīrtana. As soon as Prabhupāda turned the corner, I looked through my camera. At that time, we had very few photographs of him. There were actually a few black and white photos. In those days, photographs of Śrīla Prabhupāda were something very rare, very special. If a devotee had such a photograph, he would hold it dear and treasure it as the apple of his eye. Sometimes during japa, if some devotee had this photograph, the devotees would sit in a circle around it. 

I really wanted to take a picture of Prabhupāda so that I could have a personal photo. Prabhupāda went around the corner, and I looked through the camera. I saw Prabhupāda and thought, "Prabhupāda looks exactly like the picture." Meanwhile, the kīrtana was so noisy around me. The devotees, especially those who had received the association of Prabhupāda before, all just cried. They fell to the ground, to the floor, and cried very loudly. I looked back, and I remember my spiritual brother, Bahulāśva. He stood next to me. When I joined, he was the only hero to me because he was the leader of saṅkīrtana. He was a very good kīrtanīya. He was a preacher, a wonderful leader indeed. I thought he was such a powerful devotee, but he was lying on the floor shouting, "Prabhupāda! Prabhupāda!" I thought, "Why don't I cry? I probably have so many material desires in my heart that I don't cry when I see my spiritual master." For the first time, I put my camera down and also bowed down on the floor. 

I remember Prabhupāda came to where the devotees were, and he hugged Kīrtanānanda Mahārāja. He stroked Bhagavān dāsa’s head. It was a very warm meeting. He sat down. I don't remember exactly, but we took him a short distance and sat him down in a chair. We sat around him, and he began to give a lecture. I was simply shocked to see him. I had been meditating on him for many months. I had already been a devotee for several months. I saw him in front of me. It was amazing. 

But I remember one of his lectures. Over the years, I had met with the godbrothers of Kīrtanānanda Mahārāja, such as Praghosa. I didn't know them then, but we reminisced about this first visit of Prabhupāda. We all remembered one thing—the same sentence that Prabhupāda said in the lecture. He said, "Please believe me when I tell you that you are not the body." That was imprinted in my head and in my heart; it convinced me that I am not the body, I am the soul. I had read this often in Prabhupāda's books and heard it in lectures, but when Prabhupāda looked at us, he leaned over and said, "Please believe me when I tell you that you are not the body." That was a powerful influence. As Prabhupāda said, chanting Hare Kṛṣṇa is always for our benefit, but it is especially powerful when we hear it from the lips of a pure devotee.

### The Visit of Young Vaiṣṇava dāsa

I remember the first morning when Prabhupāda came down to give the lecture. Bhagavān dāsa’s wife, Bāmanī, had just given birth to her first child, Vaiṣṇava dāsa. She was standing next to Prabhupāda’s vyāsāsana. Prabhupāda came down the stairs in the Detroit temple. We were having a kīrtana. I was standing next to Kṛṣṇabhāminī, and her son, Vaiṣṇava, had small karatālas. He was probably less than two years old—a year and a half, maybe even a year old. He had such small karatālas, but he played them so masterfully. I'm not exaggerating. He played these karatālas beautifully and in rhythm with the kīrtana. 

Prabhupāda walked by and saw this. He stopped. He sat on the vyāsāsana, and instead of taking a karatāla and starting to lead, he started talking about this boy. "Look, this boy, he plays the karatālas wonderfully. This means that he was a devotee in a past life." I remember Kṛṣṇabhāminī was smiling from ear to ear. Mothers like to hear such things about their children. She was happy. Prabhupāda gave a lecture where he quoted the *Bhagavad-gītā* about the unsuccessful yogī who is born again either on the heavenly planets or in a family of rich people. He said, "It is better to be born in the family of a brāhmaṇa devotee. These children are special souls. They need to be taken good care of." It somehow imprinted in my mind.

Later that day, I was a gṛhastha at the time. I had a son who was one and a half years old. He had just started talking. He was sitting on my shoulders. I was chanting japa around the castle. Prabhupāda’s apartment was upstairs. Prabhupāda came out and looked out the window. I was walking, chanting japa with my son, Gaurasakti, on my shoulders. Gaurasakti saw Prabhupāda at the window. I thought he was going to fall off. "Hold on!" I shouted to him. And Prabhupāda waved at him. That evening, when he came out of his apartment, he told about this incident. There was a little boy walking with his father around the castle, and he saw me and began to shout, "Prabhupāda! Prabhupāda!" He said, "This is Kṛṣṇa consciousness. These children... do you know who these children are? Maybe they have some connection with a past life." I thought, "Oh, my son." He said, "These children, they are so joyful when they see me. So, they must have some kind of connection from a past life."

### Seeking Initiation

I don't remember how many days Prabhupāda was there, but I wanted to take initiation. I had been a devotee for eight months already. Bhagavān had a strict rule: none of the devotees he recommended for initiation left early. He was very strict. You had to wait a whole year. This was unusual at that time, because devotees were getting initiation after six, four, or even one month. Sometimes devotees would just walk into the temple, and the devotees would immediately tell him, "You must get initiation from a pure devotee." He would sit at the yajña, and then he would leave. I wanted to get initiation, but I didn't get any recommendations. I thought, "I want Prabhupāda to know that I am his disciple. I want to become his dedicated disciple." 

When Prabhupāda was leaving the Detroit temple, he went to the car. I thought, "I need to donate something to Prabhupāda." I heard something about the Bhaktivedanta Foundation, about the BBT—Bhaktivedanta Book Trust. At that time, it was called a foundation, a book foundation; it had just been started. I ran upstairs. When I joined the temple, I donated all my money. I gave about $3,000 to the temple, and the temple president allowed me to keep $10. I ran, got an envelope, and got out the $10 I had been keeping for a rainy day. It was a lot for me, an amount to buy something I couldn't get in the temple. I put it in the envelope and wrote a little letter: "Śrīla Prabhupāda, please accept my obeisances. Glory to you. Thank you so much for coming to Detroit. You were here for a very short time, but I learned so much during your visit. I have become very attached to you. I am very sorry to see you go. I will miss you very much."

I sealed the envelope and ran downstairs. Prabhupāda was just about to get into the car. I shouted, "Śrīla Prabhupāda!" I shouted very loudly. I was at the door, and he was 10 or 15 meters away from me. Everyone who had gathered stopped. Prabhupāda looked. I said, "I have a letter for you." I bowed and gave him the letter. Prabhupāda took it. This short meeting with Śrīla Prabhupāda made a very big impression on me. I saw how he stuffed it into his kurta. I thought, "My God, it will fall out of his hand, will he read my letter?" And then the car drove off. I basically forgot about this incident. I thought Prabhupāda was very busy and would not answer this letter.

### The Letter from Prabhupāda

Two weeks later, a letter arrived in the mail. In those days, when a letter arrived from Śrīla Prabhupāda, it was a special event. The President of the Temple received the letter, but he did not say who it was addressed to. We all gathered; there was a reception room in the temple, and someone blew a conch shell and shouted: "A letter from Śrīla Prabhupāda! A letter from Śrīla Prabhupāda!" We dropped everything we were doing. Some were working in the kitchen, some were cleaning the temple, some were preparing books for saṅkīrtana—we simply dropped everything and ran to the reception room. We all sat there, anticipating a few drops of nectar from Śrīla Prabhupāda. We didn't think this letter was for some special occasion; we thought everything Prabhupāda said was for all of us, that it applied to all of us. The devotees simply hung on every word of this letter.

Bhagavān said, "We received a letter from Śrīla Prabhupāda today. This letter is addressed to Bhakta Brian. Prabhupāda answered my letter." He said, "Bhakta Brian, you can come out and read the letter." I stood up, my hands shaking. I don't remember exactly because I lost the letter, but it said something like this: "My dear Bhakta Brian, please accept my blessings. I thank you very much for your donation of $10 for my book collection. I have heard from your temple leaders that you are making very good progress in Kṛṣṇa consciousness. I kindly request you to always follow in the footsteps of the advanced devotees, and in due course I will very happily accept you as my disciple." 

I was very glad; I was simply happy that the spiritual master agreed to give me initiation. Over time, this gave me a lot of spiritual strength. A few months later, Prabhupāda wrote a letter to Bhagavān, wanting him to go to Europe and help in running the center in Paris. There was a new temple in Paris. Hari-vilāsa, Locanānanda, and Bhagavan were there. There were a few devotees, and Prabhupāda felt that there should be some GBC there to organize the preaching.

### Traveling with the Guru

One day, Bhagavān said he would soon go to France. He wondered who would go with him and help him. I heard this and raised my hand. I had already traveled to Europe. He said, "Do you know how to travel?" I said, "Of course, I've been to France, Germany, and Spain. Just take me; I'll organize everything." He said, "Okay, you can go." We were already quite close, but he said, "I have one condition: you must get tickets on the same plane that Prabhupāda is flying on." Because just at that time, after a few weeks, Prabhupāda was flying to London, and we wanted to be on the same plane. He said, "If you get tickets for that plane, you can go with me." 

I got on the phone and found out from the devotees what flight Prabhupāda was on. I called the airline and spoke to a travel agent. I asked, "Do you have A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami on your list of passengers?" She said, "Yes, there is. The name of his companion was also there." I said, "I'd like two seats on this plane." They told me, "Sorry, all the seats are sold out." I was heartbroken. "Is there really nothing that can be done? This is my spiritual teacher." She asked, "Who is this?" I said, "My guru. My spiritual teacher. Like in Christianity, for example, you have Jesus Christ, and he is the most precious to you." The woman said to me, "Oh, just a second, I'll talk to my boss." After two minutes, she came back and said, "Sir, we have arranged everything. You can fly with your guru on the same plane. We have two seats for you."

A few days later, we went to New York. The temple was on Henry Street. All I remember about that street is that Prabhupāda gave initiation there. All these great devotees I had heard about were there, like Bharadvāja. Indradyumna Mahārāja was there; he was doing harināma on the street every day in New York. I remember Prabhupāda's lecture, and Bhavananda made such a colorful vyāsāsana for Prabhupāda. I was impressed. But when Prabhupāda started to sit on it, he started to slide off. I wanted to run and get Prabhupāda’s pillow; it was difficult for him to sit on it. Then it was time to go to the airport for the flight. It was me, Bhagavān, Śrīla Prabhupāda, and Pradyumna.

### The Airport Incident

We got in the car and left a little earlier, so we managed to avoid the traffic jam. By the time we arrived, most of the devotees were stuck in traffic because the whole temple was going to the airport to see Prabhupāda off. Right after Prabhupāda arrived, we went through passport control. By that time, all the devotees had just arrived—a huge crowd, hundreds of people. They wanted to see Śrīla Prabhupāda for the last time. 

I was standing next to Prabhupāda, and the security officer said, "I want to see what's inside this white bag." Prabhupāda always carried a white bag with him; he kept some of his valuables there—glasses, shorts, some documents, medicines. I always wanted to see what he was carrying in that bag. The officer said to Prabhupāda, "Open this bag." Prabhupāda was having difficulty with the locks; it was an old Indian bag. The man became rude to Prabhupāda. "Open the bag!" I leaned over him and said, "If you say that to my spiritual master again, I will smash your face." 

The man recoiled and said, "But I thought you were following the principle of non-violence." I said, "Well, in such cases, when you are talking to a pure devotee, you must observe proper etiquette." He said, "Okay, boy, open the bag then." I turned and opened it with ease. I was shocked when I looked into the bag at these personal things—the pen, the glasses, and so on. The officer said, "Excuse me, I want to see." I turned it to him, he said, "Okay, thank you," and we continued on our way. 

We were the first two passengers to board the plane. It was the first time I was alone with Prabhupāda. Prabhupāda turned around and said, "In this material world, people do not have any good qualities, because they associate with the guṇas of nature. Do you understand, Indradyumna?" I thought, "Prabhupāda is talking to me." I said, "Yes." We walked a little further, and he stopped again and said, "You see, they are fighting on the mental level, they have no Kṛṣṇa consciousness, all bad qualities. Do you understand, Indradyumna?" I said, "Yes." Then, before we reached the plane, Prabhupāda stopped and said, "Here, look. Because they associate with the modes of nature, they go down, down. But if we can restore their Kṛṣṇa consciousness, they will get all the good qualities. You see?" I said, "Yes, now I understand." Hearing those instructions three times, they entered my heart. 

### Mahā-prasāda

We entered the plane and sat down. It was just the two of us. I was just chanting Hare Kṛṣṇa so that Prabhupāda would think I was chanting well. Prabhupāda was looking out the window. Someone had passed me oranges for Prabhupāda in the crowd. I set up the table, cut the oranges with a small plastic knife, and offered them to Prabhupāda. He began to suck out the juice; his jaws were making such a noise. Then he finished the three oranges, sucked them dry, and put them back. 

He said to me, "So, Indradyumna, will you accept the prasāda?" I was a neophyte, so I said, "Prabhupāda, what prasāda? There is no more prasāda." Prabhupāda looked at me. "There is mahā-prasāda." I was at the peak of happiness that my spiritual master ordered me to take his remnants. I was in bliss. I took those orange peels, put them in my mouth, and began to chew. Naturally, they were very sour; my lips began to burn. But I thought, "This is mahā." I ate the second, the third. Tears were already flowing from my eyes. Mahā-prasāda.

### Charlie Chaplin

The plane took off. After some time, they asked to close the windows to show a movie. I thought, "This is Māyā. My God, Śrīla Prabhupāda will have to watch this movie." They started showing a Charlie Chaplin comedy. Prabhupāda really liked it. He laughed. I laughed too. We all sat there, watching this movie, and then it ended. I started thinking: "But we're not supposed to watch movies, are we? What is going on?" 

I leaned over to Pradyumna, who was on the other side of Prabhupāda. I said, "Pradyumna, can I talk to you? It's a very important question. Let's meet at the toilet." We went to the passage. I said, "We shouldn't watch a movie. What should I even think about?" He said, "I don't know. Maybe we should ask Prabhupāda." Eventually, he agreed to ask. He went to Prabhupāda and they talked. Finally, Pradyumna came back with a big smile. He said, "Yes, as a rule, he said, 'We don't watch movies, but Charlie Chaplin, his humor is very original, and Kṛṣṇa is the source of all original things.'" Of course, we know that when Prabhupāda was a boy, he liked those funny films, that innocent humor of Charlie Chaplin.

### Arriving in London

We flew to London. I was very worried because I had heard so much about Śyāmasundara, Mukunda, Ajanaka, and Gurudāsa. I expected a huge, lavish reception. When we landed, one of the bags—a very important bag with all his books and the commentaries of the ācāryas that he used for translations—did not arrive. We waited and waited. Everyone had already left. There was a big kīrtana in the waiting room, and there was no bag. Śyāmasundara came to the airport to meet us, very upset. He said, "Someone has to stay here and wait for this bag." I tried to hide, but he said, "Prabhupāda, Indradyumna can stay and he will bring this bag to the temple." Prabhupāda said, "Yes." I thought, "Oh, I'm going to miss this appointment now."

I waited for about an hour and a half. In the end, they found the bag. It was a huge, heavy suitcase filled with books. I put it on a cart and went outside. It was raining and very cold. I pulled up in a taxi and told the driver, "Bury Place 7." He asked, "Are you okay, man?" I said, "No. I missed my spiritual teacher's appointment." "What? Whom?" "My spiritual teacher. I am a devotee of Hare Kṛṣṇa." The driver replied, "Well, my condolences."

We arrived at the temple. I dragged the suitcase up the stairs. A devotee opened the door with a huge smile. "Hare Kṛṣṇa, who are you?" "I am Indradyumna dāsa. I came from Śrīla Prabhupāda. I had to wait for his bag." I went in, hoping for some prasāda, a nice feast. But all the cardboard trays were empty. I asked, "Is there any śāka left?" "Oh, you missed the feast. You should have arrived a couple of hours earlier. It was such a wonderful reception." 

I had to drag the suitcase up several steep levels. I was tired, hungry, and my soul felt empty. I saw a sign on the door: "His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda." I didn't even knock. I just walked in. I was dragging the suitcase when Nandakumāra, who was serving Śrīla Prabhupāda in London, shouted, "Hey, be careful, you are going to collide with Prabhupāda!" I stopped, turned around, and I was right in front of Prabhupāda. He stood there chanting, "Hare Kṛṣṇa." I said, "Oh, Śrīla Prabhupāda," and I fell to the floor and began to bow. 

Suddenly, I felt a slap on my back. I couldn't tell if he was doing it out of love or anger. I can still feel that slap here. After a few seconds, I stood up. Nandakumāra was standing there with his mouth open. I asked, "Did something happen?" He said, "Prabhupāda slapped you on the back." "I know." "He pinned you so hard." "Yes, I know," I said. "And what did he say?" Nandakumāra smiled and said, "Oh, he said wonderful things. He told you that so much effort has to be put into this material world, but when I take you back home to Godhead, everything will be easy and wonderful and sublime."

### Personal Instructions

Often, as time goes by, difficulties come to us in Kṛṣṇa consciousness. The movement is spreading, we are faced with opposition, we get tired. In such difficult days, I remember these words: "We put so much effort in this world, but when I take you to Godhead, everything will be easy." We fight in this world to serve Prabhupāda and to promote the saṅkīrtana movement, because at the end, Prabhupāda will take us all back to Godhead.

After we arrived in London, we went to Prabhupāda's room. He called us because he wanted to inspire us, as we were going to Paris. We knocked, and Nandakumāra opened the door. Prabhupāda was sitting at a low table. We bowed down, and Prabhupāda preached about the importance of the saṅkīrtana movement. He said he was very grateful that we were going there to help. Then he looked at me and said, "My instruction to you: Preach boldly and have faith in the holy name." 

Prabhupāda said, "I will give you something to inspire you." He opened the drawer and handed me one of his dhotīs. I pressed it to my heart and thought, "Now I have a treasure. I have the instruction to preach boldly, to have faith in the holy name. And I have his dhotī." Even though I was a gṛhastha, I always wore it as a cādar. 

For the blessings of Prabhupāda, we went to Paris. When we entered the temple, the altar had just fallen down. There were some cardboard boxes and images of the Pañca-tattva. The devotees performed a big kīrtana. I thought, "Oh my God, where did Prabhupāda send us? This is an old building, it's cold, and the altar has fallen." But the kīrtana was very extravagant. After two weeks, Prabhupāda arrived in Paris. We received him there in that small temple. I remember one morning walk with Śrīla Prabhupāda. The French have wonderful little houses with gardens. I was impressed by these gardens, but Prabhupāda was not. He said, "These French are great experts in sensual pleasure.""
