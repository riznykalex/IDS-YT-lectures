Those three stages up until adulthood, as you remember, are called childhood—*kaumāra*, which is from birth to five years of age—then boyhood—*pauganda*, which is from six years to ten years old—and then His youth—*kaiśora*, which lasts from 11 years to 15 years old. Kṛṣṇa, Śrī Rūpa Gosvāmī for me. So now, let us go deeper and learn about these three stages of Kṛṣṇa’s growing up. There’s a lot of nectar.

### The Childhood Stage: *Kaumāra* (0–5 Years)

At the beginning of His childhood, that very first stage of *kaumāra*, I was reading that Kṛṣṇa is very chubby and His limbs are described as tender as blue lotuses, and the edges of His eyes are white, and His small teeth are just beginning to show. The ācāryas say that during this time, Mother Yaśodā cannot take her eyes off her son as He sucks His big toe, and while sometimes crying and sometimes laughing, He throws His legs up in the air like you see little toddlers do when they’re lying on their back. Śrī Rūpa Gosvāmī writes about this early stage of Kṛṣṇa’s life again in *Padyāvalī* very beautifully; in text 107 he writes:

"When can I see Kṛṣṇa, lovely and alluring like a blooming blackwood, His lotus face pressed into His mother’s breast while He wiggles His lotus toes?"

During this period of Kṛṣṇa’s life, Mother Yaśodā, I was reading, covers Kṛṣṇa with protective charms. Every morning she hangs a tiger’s claw around His neck, she ties a cord around His waist and a string around His wrist, then she paints tilaka marks on His body, and then she smears mascara around Kṛṣṇa’s eyes to protect Him from the glare of the sun. Now, towards the middle of this first stage of childhood, it’s described that Kṛṣṇa occasionally crawls about, but when He reaches the end part of this period of His childhood, He becomes very proficient in crawling. At that time, He pleases His parents with the charming way He speaks in broken language as He begins to form words. 

I was reading that perhaps the most notable feature of this period is Kṛṣṇa’s discovery that He can have foodstuff other than His mother’s breast milk. He’s getting a little older, so He’s discovering He can have things like butter and yogurt. The ācāryas say He relishes these things with great enthusiasm, although they say He can’t have them all the time; He’s still dependent on His mother’s breast milk. During this period, Mother Yaśodā often takes Kṛṣṇa in her lap and showers Him with her love. There’s a very beautiful description of Mother Yaśodā again in *Padyāvalī* by Rūpa Gosvāmī—I think it’s verse 128. Poetry often gives us the essence and the mood of something, so Rūpa Gosvāmī writes about Mother Yaśodā: "Her lotus-navel child in her lap, her complexion like new clouds, garbed in multi-colored clothes; constantly I bow to Yaśodā, who delights the whole world."

Now, during the final stage of Kṛṣṇa’s childhood years (one to five), Kṛṣṇa is by this time—you know, like four or five—on His feet. He’s walking about, and at this stage, He and His friends begin to herd calves and play games in the fields just outside Gokula. Because they’re still pretty young, they don’t go very far; they remain under the watchful eyes of the older cowherd men, but it’s good training. Ācāryas write: "It’s so beautiful. Dressed in a short *dhoti*, He carries a stick in His hand in imitation of the cowherd men, and wears a peacock feather in His hair, which is braided and tied in a topknot." Hearing all these things, you just get this very beautiful, real impression of Kṛṣṇa. We’re hearing what He looks like, and we become so attracted.

The ācāryas go on to say: "There in the nearby pasturing grounds, Kṛṣṇa eats His lunch, not at home as previously, but with His friends." When I’m reading these last months, I find so many descriptions of this noon pastime of Kṛṣṇa in my readings where He takes prasādam with His friends each day; it’s always a big thing. All this eating is done in a very fun and loving way with lots of joking. I mean, the cowherd boys and Kṛṣṇa, they spent a lot of time joking. Prabhupāda refers to this joking mood in a wonderful purport in *Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam*, 10th Canto, 13th Chapter, verse number 11. I just came across this; he writes:

"When Kṛṣṇa was eating with His cowherd boyfriends, a certain bumblebee came there to take part in the eating. Then Kṛṣṇa joked, 'Why have you come to disturb My brāhmaṇa friend Madhu-maṅgala? You want to kill a brāhmaṇa? This is not good.'" 

Prabhupāda continues: "All the boys would laugh and enjoy speaking such joking words while eating. Thus, the inhabitants of the higher planets were astonished at how the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who eats only when yajña is offered, was now eating like an ordinary child with His friends in the forest."

### The Boyhood Stage: *Paugaṇḍa* (6–10 Years)

Now we’ll move on to the next stage of Kṛṣṇa’s growing up, His boyhood age called *paugaṇḍa*, and this again is from six years old to 10 years old. In this phase of life, I was reading that Kṛṣṇa ventures out a little further into the many forests of Vṛndāvana to herd the cows with His friends. While He’s doing that—herding cows with His friends—the ācāryas say He’s also mastering different games with His friends, learning certain wrestling holds as well as specific dancing steps. During this particular period, Kṛṣṇa is wearing a very short silken *dhoti*, and He’s adorned with *guñjā-berī* necklaces, a peacock feather crown, jasmine garlands, and flowers on His ears. At this particular period of His growing up, He’s much more mature by this time. I read a very nice passage wherein it is described that after having visited Vṛndāvana when Kṛṣṇa was just a baby, a group of traveling minstrels—those who travel and play music as a profession and to entertain people—returned to Vraja during this boyhood stage of Kṛṣṇa’s life. They came through Vṛndāvana when He was just a little baby, but they came again some years later when Kṛṣṇa was a little bit older, in this *paugaṇḍa* stage, and it’s described that they were amazed to see how beautiful Kṛṣṇa had become.

There’s a passage where they reveal how they’re so amazed: "O Kṛṣṇa," these minstrels said, "Your abdomen is like a beautiful *āvarta*, your throat now has three lines of a conch, and your lips have stolen the redness of rubies. Like a moon on earth, Your attractive features give bliss to the eyes of Your friends as they do to ours as well."

In the middle stage of this boyhood period, Kṛṣṇa becomes expert in His duty of herding cows and calves to these distant pastures. As He gets a little older, He starts wearing a very colorful turban decorated with a golden rope for tying calves, and this turban is also bristling with flower buds and peacock feathers. I also read that at this time, He holds a black, gold-capped staff in His hand for three purposes: to control the cows, to play-fight with His friends, and it is said, to declare Himself the future heir to Vraja’s throne.

Now His facial features become a little finer at this stage, and people begin to notice His raised nose and neck and His rounded chin and cheeks. But the most striking component of Kṛṣṇa’s beauty at this age, I was reading, is the sweetness that emanates from His form. The final stage of this boyhood period is marked by His changing mental disposition and His locks of hair, which look like bumblebees that dance playfully on His forehead, one ācārya describes. At this point, His hair is so long that it reaches to His hips when braided. Also, the Vrajavāsīs notice that His shoulders become wider, they note that His turban is always a little tilted, they note that He’s wearing saffron tilaka, and that He’s always twirling a lotus in His hand. It’s mentioned that all these things together become the talk of the young Vraja Gopis in Vṛndāvana. I found a nice verse in *Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu* (3.3.75) that reveals how the Gopis start talking about Kṛṣṇa; it’s Kṛṣṇa’s friend Subala speaking:

"Speaking to Kṛṣṇa, 'Okay, your attractive clothing, the tilted turban, the played-with lotus in your hand, the golden tilaka on your forehead, and the dot of musk are making me, Subala, completely dizzy. How much more will this affect the naturally susceptible Gopis?'"

### The Youthful Stage: *Kaiśora* (11–15 Years)

Now we move on to the third and final stage of Kṛṣṇa’s growing up, His youth, *kaiśora*, which lasts from 11 to 15 years. This was a very significant development in Kṛṣṇa’s pastimes. This particular stage, 11 to 15, you could say is the most important of the three primary stages. I say that because this is actually what Lord Caitanya said regarding this particular third stage in His growing up, as described in *Caitanya-caritāmṛta* when Lord Caitanya was having a meeting with the devotee Raghupati Upādhyāya. At that meeting, Lord Caitanya asked:

"Of the three ages of Kṛṣṇa—childhood, boyhood, and fresh youth—which do you consider the best?" 
Raghupati Upādhyāya replied, "Fresh youth is the best age." 
So next, the Lord inquired, "Amongst all the mellows, which do you consider the best?" 
Raghupati Upādhyāya replied, "The mellow of amorous love is superior." 
Who he concluded, he said, "You have certainly given first-class conclusions." 

So, there’s a lot of detailed information about this particular stage of Kṛṣṇa’s growing up, 11 to 15, *kaiśora*. The first stage of this youth, when He’s around 11 or 12, is a change in Kṛṣṇa’s appearance. For example, I was reading His bodily hue becomes dark and glossy, and it’s described more like the splendor of a sapphire than the color of a blue lotus. How deep do our ācāryas go! The ācāryas say that "the soft, fine hair appearing on His limbs adds to both the darkness and texture of His complexion." They also say that His eyebrows become thicker and start to resemble Cupid’s legendary bow, and then Kṛṣṇa takes advantage of this change by shooting arrow-like glances from His bow at the young Vraja Gopis. Finally, whereas the whites of His eyes were previously clear, their corners are now tinged with a distinctive reddish hue. 

Kṛṣṇa’s appearance is further enhanced by a new manner of dressing. At this age, He starts dressing like a dancing actor on a stage; I remember that from reading the *Kṛṣṇa* book. With elaborate knee-length *vaijayantī* garlands—remember a *vaijayantī* garland is five types of flowers—He starts wearing these as He dresses like a dancing actor. It’s also stated—and this is really interesting—that while both the peacock feather and flute are not new companions in Kṛṣṇa’s adventures, they are now proud emblems of His youth. This is pure nectar. Another interesting development is Kṛṣṇa’s ability to play very sweet, mind-enchanting flute melodies, even more enchanting than ever before. He becomes very proficient at playing His flute, and I also came across the following statement: according to some Gopis, this is due to Kṛṣṇa having taken flute lessons from Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī. 

A few other bodily characteristics of Kṛṣṇa appear at this age. It’s described that the sharpness of His fingernails becomes sharper and His teeth appear a little more reddish. I thought that was interesting; it’s described that instead of being round like full moons, Kṛṣṇa’s fingernails appear like a line of sharp arrows. And His teeth become a little reddish as a result of chewing *pāṇ*. It’s at this point of His maturing that the Gopis begin to speak about Kṛṣṇa’s transition into youth, whereby they can start having more amorous pastimes with Him. 

As Kṛṣṇa enters His 13th year, His arms become muscular, His chest becomes very broad, and His thighs become very clearly masculine, as described by one ācārya. He says, "The wealth of His sweetness is visible in the increased luster of His face, His endearing soft smile, and His restless eyes that constantly agitate the minds of young girls by hinting at amorous intentions." Just as Kṛṣṇa perfects His flute playing in the first stage of youth, He now perfects His singing, enchanting the world and inducing chaste women to break their vows of marriage. 

Now, what is very significant is that at this age, Kṛṣṇa begins *rāsa-līlā* with the Vraja Gopis of Vṛndāvana. At around 15, it’s described that all His bodily features become more alluring than previously, and whereas before you could see three lines on His neck, now three lines are clearly manifest on His abdomen near His navel. When Kṛṣṇa arrives at the age of 16 years, He stops growing; He never ages beyond that. Essentially, He remains ever youthful. For the Gopis, this is the age of Kṛṣṇa that they prefer the most. One ācārya says, "Kṛṣṇa’s paramount sweetness at this age defeats Cupid’s arrows, makes women lose all composure, is a playground for the 64 amorous arts, and causes an awakening of amorous love not relishable during His previous ages."

The effect of Kṛṣṇa’s eternal youth at 16 years of age on the Gopis is described by Śrīla Rūpa Gosvāmī in *Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu* (2.1.333) in the following way. The Gopis are speaking about Kṛṣṇa: 

"O Kṛṣṇa, today Your age of youth in the role of guru is teaching the golden Gopis the art of whispering in each other’s ears, making verses of praise for messengers, wile, cleverness, and cheating their husbands, practicing sneaking to the forest at night, and deafness to the words of elders, and finally, being rapt in hearing the flute sound." 

Hare Kṛṣṇa. I’m going pretty deep there. That’s all on the transcendental platform—the love of the Vrajavāsīs for Kṛṣṇa and Kṛṣṇa’s love for the Vrajavāsīs, just relishing seeing their beloved as He goes through these three stages of childhood up until He’s an adult, 16 years old, all attractive forever in that particular age.

### Lord Balarāma

So, before concluding, I think it would be appropriate to mention and describe a little bit Kṛṣṇa’s brother and constant companion through all of His youthful pastimes, and of course, that’s Lord Balarāma. In ISKCON, we have many sets of Kṛṣṇa-Balarāma Deities. The two of Them grew up through this youthful stage of Kṛṣṇa’s life, and Balarāma, being a little older than Kṛṣṇa, of course, we’ve described previously that He’s always in a mood to protect Kṛṣṇa and serve Kṛṣṇa. He has that dual *rasa*; He’s the older brother, so He’s always protecting Kṛṣṇa. But because Kṛṣṇa is the original Supreme Personality of Godhead and Balarāma is the first expansion, Balarāma considers Himself subservient, although Prabhupāda said Kṛṣṇa and Balarāma, the only difference is that Kṛṣṇa is bluish-black like a monsoon cloud and Balarāma is white like an autumn cloud; otherwise, they’re the same. 

His complexion is whitish, although sometimes—I was reading—his complexion is like shining crystal. And of course, Balarāma wears blue clothing, which I read inspires many of Kṛṣṇa’s friends to also wear blue cloth. There is fire seeing Balarāma in blue, so Kṛṣṇa’s friends, they also put on blue sometimes. Balarāma’s hair is tied in a topknot, priceless earrings swing against His cheeks, His neck is graced with forest-flower garlands and jeweled necklaces, and His arms are thick with bracelets and armlets. The ācāryas in one particular section concluded: "Upon His feet tinkle splendid *cintāmaṇi* anklets that have acquired their philosopher’s-stone nature from their constant resting place on His feet." We all know Him as the emblem of youthful luster, as Kṛṣṇa’s protector, as the greatest reservoir of nectarean mellows. Obeisances to Him, Dauji, Lord Balarāma. Kṛṣṇa-Balarāma.
