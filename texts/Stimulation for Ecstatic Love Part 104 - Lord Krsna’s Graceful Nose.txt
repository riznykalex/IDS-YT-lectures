But as we mentioned last week, of all His sweetness, Kṛṣṇa's face is the sweetest, and we gave many references for that. One proof of this statement is that—I was thinking—any devotee who has the opportunity to see that face of Kṛṣṇa is never satiated by that darśana. Such a devotee wants to look upon the face of the Lord forever and forever more. In fact, in Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Madhya-līlā 21.132, we hear: "If by devotional service one gets the results of pious activities and sees Lord Kṛṣṇa's face, what can he relish with only two eyes? His greed and thirst increase twofold by seeing the nectarian face of Kṛṣṇa. Due to his inability to sufficiently drink that nectar, he becomes very unhappy and criticizes the Creator for not having given more than two eyes." You remember how the gopīs complain like that.

Now, going deeper and deeper, the ācāryas say that the sweetest part of Kṛṣṇa's face is what? His smile! We touched a little bit on this in the last lecture, but there is more. In Kṛṣṇa-karṇāmṛta, verse 92, by Bilvamaṅgala Ṭhākura, Kṛṣṇa's body, face, and smile have been described as sweet, sweeter, and sweetest. His body, His face, and His smile have been described as sweet, sweeter, and sweetest. What is the verse?

"The body of the Lord is very, very sweet; His face is even sweeter; but ah, His gentle smile, smelling of honey, is the sweetest!"

Let us recite that again:

"The body of the Lord is very sweet; His face is even sweeter; but ah, His gentle smile, smelling of honey, is the sweetest!"

Now, Lord Caitanya Mahāprabhu agreed to this. Mahāprabhu agreed to this, for in Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Madhya-līlā 21, verses 139 to 140, absorbed in the mood of Śrīmatī Rādhārāṇī, the Lord said:

"Of all His features, the beauty of Kṛṣṇa's smile is the sweetest, for it is like a full moon distributing its rays in all ten directions—throughout the material world, in Vaikuṇṭha, and beyond in Goloka. The fragrant illumination of Kṛṣṇa's smile is like camphor. It permeates the sweetness of His lips, which He then blows into His flute and transforms into divine vibrations that enter into space."

Wow! And later in Caitanya-caritāmṛta, Antya-līlā 17.44, Lord Caitanya again glorifies the smile of Kṛṣṇa's face. He says: "Kṛṣṇa's camphor-like smile also mixes with His pleasing speech to produce a mysterious, deeply meaningful sound that awakens all kinds of divine mellows in the hearts of those who hear it." All that from the mouth of Lord Caitanya Himself!
